# Case Study: Grupo de Capoeira Angola Irmãos Guerreiros (GCAIG)

**Date**: 2025-11-19
**Founded**: 1983
**Location**: Taboão da Serra, São Paulo (periphery), Brazil (international presence)
**Style**: Capoeira Angola (traditional)
**Scale**: International (Brazil, Portugal, Germany, Austria, Poland, Slovakia, Norway - 10+ locations)

---

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

**Limited Scope**: This case study examines ONE group out of hundreds (possibly thousands) of Capoeira organizations worldwide.

**Not Representative**: Observations here reflect only GCAIG's specific structure and history. Other groups may have completely different organizational models.

**Preliminary Research**: All insights are preliminary and based on publicly available information. Community verification is essential before drawing any general conclusions about Capoeira organizational patterns.

**Name Disambiguation**: Only one group named "Grupo de Capoeira Angola Irmãos Guerreiros" (GCAIG) was found during research. No naming conflicts identified.

## Founding History

### The Founders (Family Founding - Four Co-Founders)

**Founded 1983** in Taboão da Serra (periphery of Greater São Paulo) by a Bahian family:

1. **Mestre Baixinho** (father, from Bahia)
2. **Mestre Marrom** (son, started capoeira 1978 at age 8)
3. **Guerreiro** (Baixinho's brother, uncle to Marrom)
4. **Macete** (Baixinho's brother, uncle to Marrom)

### Founding Context

**Family migration**: Bahian family "recently arrived in São Paulo" before 1983
- Originally from Bahia (cradle of Capoeira)
- Moved to periphery of São Paulo (Taboão da Serra)
- Built house in 1970s that would become group headquarters

**Name meaning**: "Irmãos Guerreiros" = "Warrior Brothers"
- References the brothers (Baixinho, Guerreiro, Macete)
- Emphasizes familial bond and warrior spirit
- Collective identity rather than individual founder name

## Headquarters: "Senzalinha"

### Location

**Address**: Taboão da Serra, São Paulo (periphery/outskirts of Greater São Paulo)

**Neighborhood context**:
- Peripheral region
- Low housing infrastructure
- Many families live "on the margins of social inclusion"
- Senzalinha serves as **cultural hub** in marginalized community

### Physical Space

**Building**: Garage of house built by Mestre Baixinho's family in the 1970s

**Adaptation**: "adapted and decorated to create a conducive and stimulating environment for the practice of capoeira and other cultural manifestations"

**Nickname significance**: "Senzalinha" = "Little Senzala"
- Reference to Grupo Senzala (famous Rio group)
- Diminutive suffix "-inha" (affection + smaller scale)
- Headquarters often called "Senzalinha" affectionately by community

### Current Activities

- Regular capoeira angola classes
- Samba de Roda
- Maculelê
- Puxada de Rede (fishing net pulling tradition)
- Community cultural center functions
- Social integration programs

## Mission & Philosophy

### Stated Mission

**Primary objective**: "Preserve and disseminate the traditions of Capoeira Angola and other Afro-Brazilian manifestations"

**Social transformation**: "Using popular culture as an engine capable of generating social changes in the space where it is practiced and experienced"

### Core Values

**Community focus**:
- Working with children, adolescents, and adults
- Emphasis on social integration
- Providing alternatives to violence and hopelessness
- Attracting people from diverse backgrounds

**Cultural preservation**:
- Capoeira Angola (traditional style)
- Other Afro-Brazilian manifestations (Samba de Roda, Maculelê, Puxada de Rede)
- Cultural identity and resistance

**Social justice**:
- Located deliberately in peripheral/marginalized community
- Using culture as tool for social inclusion
- "Cultural hub" in area with limited infrastructure

### Group Symbolism

**Colors**: Brown and White
- **Brown**: Represents earth as source of nourishment and strength
- **White**: Represents peace

## Organizational Structure

### Leadership Model

**Dual coordination** (Brazil + Europe):
- **Brazil**: Mestre Baixinho + Mestre Marrom (father-son leadership)
- **Europe**: Mestre Perna (primary) + Contra-mestre Kenneth ("right hand")

**Family-based founding**: Distinguishes from most groups
- Father + son + two uncles = 4 co-founders
- Second generation (Marrom) involved from childhood
- Continuity through family succession

### Organizational Model

**Nucleus network with dual regional coordination**:
- **São Paulo headquarters** (Senzalinha): Mestre Baixinho, Mestre Marrom
- **European hub** (Bremen - Cazuá): Mestre Perna, CM Kenneth
- **Local núcleos**: Led by Professores, Treeneis under regional coordination

**Characteristics**:
- Semi-autonomous núcleos
- Shared identity and mission
- Two primary cultural centers (Senzalinha in São Paulo, Cazuá in Bremen, Maloca in Lisbon)
- Local instructors lead classes, regional coordinators provide oversight
- No apparent licensing/fee structure documented

### Legal Structure

**Brazil**:
- Association model (typical for cultural organizations)
- Based in Taboão da Serra, peripheral municipality

**Germany**:
- **Centro Cultural Cazuá e.V.** (registered non-profit association)
- e.V. = eingetragener Verein (German non-profit structure)
- Focus on cultural preservation and social integration
