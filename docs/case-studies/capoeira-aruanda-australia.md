**Birth Name**: José Amorim

**1997**: Came to Sydney, Australia as teacher with Grupo Capoeira Brasil
- Immigrated from Brazil to Australia
- Arrived as representative/teacher of established organization (CB)

**1997-2005**: Teaching Capoeira Brasil in Australia (8 years)

- Developed teaching experience in diaspora context

**2005**: Founded "Capoeiracre" with associates
- Named after his home state of Acre, Brazil

- Nature of split not documented (blessed vs. conflictual unknown)

**2007**: Renamed organization to "Capoeira Aruanda"
- Name change from geographic (Capoeiracre) to philosophical/cultural (Aruanda)

**2015**: Awarded Mestre belt by Mestre Boa Gente
- 10 years after founding own group
- 18 years after arriving in Australia

## The Name "Aruanda" - Cultural Significance

### Etymology and Meaning

**Origin**: "Aruanda" is a mutation of "Luanda" - the capital city of Angola, Africa.

**Historical Context**:
- Luanda was a major port from which enslaved Africans were stolen
- Represented the homeland that enslaved people hoped to return to
- Symbolizes longing for freedom and return to African roots

**In Capoeira Songs**: "Aruanda" has become associated with:
- Freedom that would come with returning home
- African cultural connection
- Resistance and liberation
- Spiritual/mythical place of freedom

**Choosing this name (2007)**: Reflects commitment to:
- African roots of capoeira
- Freedom philosophy (appropriate for independent group post-split)
- Cultural depth over geographic identification
- Connection to capoeira's resistance history

**Comparison to original name**:
- "Capoeiracre" (2005-2007): Geographic, personal connection to home state
- "Capoeira Aruanda" (2007-present): Cultural, philosophical, African connection

### Mestre Graduation Lineage - Independent Path

**Graduated by**: Mestre Boa Gente (2015)

**Mestre Boa Gente** (Vivaldo Conceição Rodrigues):
- Born May 17, 1945, Ibicaraí, South Bahia, Brazil
- **Multi-style training** (Angola AND Regional):
  1. Started with Mestre Antônio Cabeceiro (streets of Ilhéus)
  2. Trained with Mestre Gato (Escola Baiana de Capoeira Angola)
  3. Trained with Mestre Vermelho 27 (Mestre Bimba's academy, Regional)
- **Founded**: Associação de Capoeira Mestre Boa Gente (1981)
- **Independent organization** - not part of CB or other major groups

**Mestre Vermelho 27** (José Carlos Andrade Bittencourt):
```
Mestre Bimba (Regional founder, 1900-1974)
    ↓
Mestre Vermelho 27 (student from 1953, met Bimba 1952)
    - When Bimba left Salvador, Vermelho 27 took over academy
    - After Bimba died (1974), renamed to "Associação de Capoeira Mestre Bimba" (1975)
    ↓
Mestre Boa Gente (trained 1960s-1970s)
    - Also trained Angola with Mestre Gato
    - Founded own association 1981
    ↓
Mestre Borracha (graduated 2015)
```

**Significance of Independent Graduation**:
- Did NOT seek mestre title from CB founders (Boneco, Paulão Ceará, Paulinho Sabiá)
- Chose independent mestre with direct Bimba lineage
- Chose mestre with both Angola AND Regional background (Boa Gente trained both)
- Mestre Boa Gente's organization founded 1981 (predates CB by 8 years)
- Represents autonomous legitimization outside parent organization

**This pattern suggests**:
- Borracha sought independence from CB hierarchy
- Valued direct connection to Bimba lineage through Vermelho 27
- Appreciated Boa Gente's multi-style approach (Angola + Regional)
- Established separate lineage authority for Capoeira Aruanda

## Style and Approach - Contemporânea

### Classification: Contemporary Capoeira

**Official Description**: "Capoeira Aruanda is a contemporary group"

**Contemporary/Contemporânea** typically means:
- Blends elements of Angola (traditional, slow, strategic) and Regional (athletic, acrobatic, fast)
- Does not strictly adhere to one style's rules
- Modern approach while respecting tradition
- Often emphasizes acrobatics and athletic performance
- Music incorporates both styles

**Borracha's Background Suggests Contemporary**:
- 1992 achievement: Double back somersault (highly acrobatic, Regional/Contemporary emphasis)
- Trained/worked with Grupo Capoeira Brasil (Contemporary organization)
- Graduated by Mestre Boa Gente (trained both Angola AND Regional)

**Not Pure Angola**: Unlike Capoeira Mandinga Aotearoa (NZ) which shifted to pure Angola, Capoeira Aruanda maintains Contemporary approach.
