## Founder: Mestre Marcos Gytauna

### Biography

**Full Name**: Marcos Moura Freitas
**Known As**: Mestre Marcos Gytauna
**Born**: Unknown (approximately 1950s-1960s based on timeline)
**From**: São Salvador, Bahia, Brazil
**Based**: Buenos Aires, Argentina (since 1987)

### Early Training

**Started Capoeira**: 1975
**Location**: Liberdade neighborhood, São Salvador, Bahia, Brazil
**Primary Teacher**: Mestre Alfredo

**Training Environment**:
- Trained alongside childhood best friend Mestre Val Boa Morte (later founder of Capoeira Filhos da Bahia International)
- Trained at Mestre Alfredo's School in Salvador, Bahia
- "Three times champions of Bahian capoeira" (Marcos and Val together)

### Complete Lineage Chain

```
Mestre Saci + Mestre Paulo dos Anjos
(Early Bahian capoeira masters)
  ↓
Mestre Alfredo (Salvador, Bahia)
  ↓
├─ Mestre Marcos Gytauna (Marcos Moura Freitas)
│  → Argentina 1987 → Founded AAC/Oriaxé 1995
│
└─ Mestre Val Boa Morte (Edval Santos)
   → Argentina 1987-1989 → Brazil → Australia 1992 → Filhos da Bahia International

Also trained with Mestre Alfredo at same school:
- Contra-Mestre Nei Boa Morte (Val's brother, now Mestre)
- Dinho Boa Morte (Val's brother)
```

**Generational Distance**:
- 2 generations from Mestre Saci and Mestre Paulo dos Anjos
- 1 generation from Mestre Alfredo
- Contemporary of Mestre Val (Filhos da Bahia founder)

**Note on Relationship**: Mestre Marcos and Mestre Val were:
- Childhood best friends
- Trained together from 1975+ at Mestre Alfredo's school
- Three-time Bahian champions together
- Arrived Argentina together January 10, 1987
- Worked together 1987-1989
- Separated when Val returned to Brazil (end 1989)
- Maintain lifelong friendship despite separate organizations

### Arrival in Argentina (1987)

**Date**: January 10, 1987

**Invitation**: Actress Egle Martin ("La Negra"), well-known figure in Argentine culture
- Known for investigating African presence in Argentina
- Specialist in African, Brazilian, jazz rhythms fused with tango and folklore
- Created massive candombe parades with 40+ dancers/drums

**Project**: "Ritos y Candombe" (later "Ritos y Candombes III")
- Cultural showcase of Afro-Latin heritage
- Presented at disco Palladium, Buenos Aires (iconic venue)
- Earlier versions in 1983 at La Capilla
- Featured fragments of Afro culture in Latin America

**Original Plan**: Five-day visit

**What Changed**: First student, actor Adrián Ghio, and his wife encouraged Marcos to stay and dedicate himself to teaching capoeira

**Cultural Context**:
- Capoeira completely unknown in Argentina in 1987
- Argentines confused capoeira with feijoada (Brazilian stew) or caipirinha (cocktail)
- Marcos had to constantly demonstrate and educate people about what capoeira was

## Group Evolution & Organizational History

### Name: "Oriaxé"

**Meaning**: Unknown (requires verification from Yoruba or Portuguese)

**Full Organization Name**: Asociación Argentina de Capoeira (AAC) - Grupo Oriaxé / Grupo Oriaxé Internacional

### Early Period (1987-1995)

**1987-1989**: Working with Mestre Val
- Arrived together January 10, 1987
- Built their school together in Buenos Aires
- Both teaching capoeira to Argentine students
- Worked as partners in same organization

**End of 1989**: Partnership Ends
- Mestre Val returned to Brazil
- Mestre Marcos decided to stay in Argentina
- Reason for separation: Unknown (not documented as hostile)
- Val later moved to Australia (November 10, 1992) to lead Filhos da Bahia International

**1987-1995**: Marcos Continues Solo
- Taught at various locations in Buenos Aires:
  - Palermo Chico (initial location)
  - Body Gym gymnasium
  - Centro Cultural Ricardo Rojas (significant cultural venue)
- Built student base of approximately 20 students by 1995

### Official Founding (1995)

**Date**: May 14, 1995

**Organization**: **Asociación Argentina de Capoeira (AAC)**
- Official legal entity established
- Marcos Gytauna as founder and director
- Recognized as "oldest capoeira institution in Argentina"

**Initial Location**: Serrano street (now renamed Jorge Luis Borges street), Buenos Aires

**Founding Members**: Approximately 20 students

**Significance**: While Marcos had been teaching since 1987, the formal association was established in 1995, giving capoeira institutional legitimacy in Argentina

### Key Milestones

**1987**:
- Marcos and Val arrive Buenos Aires (January 10)
- First capoeira instruction in Argentina
- Performance in "Ritos y Candombe" production

**1989**:
- Mestre Val returns to Brazil (end of year)
- Marcos continues solo

**1995**:
- AAC officially founded (May 14)
- First formal capoeira institution in Argentina

**2010**:
- Hosted **17th International Capoeira Encounter** in Buenos Aires
- Marcos had been in Argentina 23 years at this point
- International recognition of Argentine capoeira community

**2017** (estimated):
- Celebrated 30 years of capoeira in Argentina (1987-2017)

**2025**:
- **30th anniversary of AAC** (1995-2025)
- Plans for celebrations with guest mestres from Brazil
- Continuing operations in Buenos Aires

### Music & Cultural Contributions

**Songs Composed** by Mestre Marcos Gytauna:
1. "Berimbau de Fe" (Berimbau of Faith)
2. "Capoeira Me Chama" (Capoeira Calls Me)
3. "Ser Capoeira" (To Be Capoeira)

These songs contribute to capoeira's musical repertoire and reflect Argentine capoeira experience.

## Cultural Practices & Philosophy

### Style Classification

**Unknown Specific Style**:
- Lineage from Bahia suggests Regional or Contemporânea (not traditional Angola)
- Mestre Alfredo's school style not documented
- Training in 1970s Salvador suggests Regional influence (post-Bimba era)
- Championship competitions suggest Regional or Contemporânea (Angola typically non-competitive)

**Likely**: Regional or Contemporânea based on:
- Bahian training in 1970s (post-Bimba systemization)
- Competitive background ("three times champions")
- Association with Mestre Alfredo (not Angola-specific lineage)

### Teaching Methodology

**Cultural Education Focus**:
- Educating Argentines about what capoeira is (initially confused with food/drinks)
- Explaining African-Brazilian heritage
- Contextualizing within larger Afro-Latin culture (connected to Egle Martin's work)

**Philosophy Emphasis**:
- "Philosophy of life" beyond physical movements
- Cultural immersion, not just martial art training
- Brazilian cultural ambassadorship

**Music Emphasis**:
- Mestre Marcos composes original capoeira songs
- Three documented compositions
- Likely emphasis on singing and instruments

### Capoeira in Argentine Context

**Challenges**:
- Complete unfamiliarity with capoeira in 1987
- Cultural gap: Brazilian art form in European-influenced Argentina
- Need to constantly demonstrate and explain
- Building community from zero

**Adaptations**:
- Teaching in Spanish (not Portuguese)
- Connecting to Argentine interest in Afro-Latin culture (via Egle Martin connection)
- Establishing institutional legitimacy (AAC as formal association)
- Long-term commitment (38 years teaching in Argentina)

**Cultural Bridge**:
- Brought Brazilian culture to Argentina
- Connected to broader Afro-Latin cultural movement
- Part of increasing Brazilian cultural presence in Argentina

## Comparison: Oriaxé vs. Filhos da Bahia (Australia)

Both organizations stem from childhood friends who trained together:

| Aspect | Oriaxé (Argentina) | Filhos da Bahia (Australia) |
|--------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Founder** | Mestre Marcos Gytauna | Mestre Val Boa Morte |
| **Relationship** | Childhood best friends, trained together, "3x Bahian champions" | |
| **Teacher** | Mestre Alfredo (same teacher) | Mestre Alfredo + Waldemar da Liberdade, Nô, Alabama, Boa Gente |
| **Training Location** | Salvador, Bahia, Brazil | Salvador, Bahia, Brazil |
| **Arrived Argentina** | January 10, 1987 (together) | January 10, 1987 (together) |
| **Worked Together** | 1987-1989 (2-3 years) | 1987-1989 (2-3 years) |
| **Separation** | End of 1989 (stayed in Argentina) | End of 1989 (returned to Brazil) |
| **Formal Founding** | AAC 1995 (Buenos Aires) | Filhos da Bahia 1983 (Salvador, Brazil) |
| **International Move** | Argentina (permanent since 1987) | Australia (November 10, 1992) |
| **Organization Name** | Asociación Argentina de Capoeira / Grupo Oriaxé | Capoeira Filhos da Bahia International |
| **Primary Location** | Buenos Aires, Argentina | Melbourne, Australia |
| **Co-Founders** | Solo (after Val left) | Partnership with Mestre Alfredo + brothers Nei & Dinho Boa Morte |
| **Current Status** | Active, 30th anniversary 2025 | Active, established network |
| **Pioneer Status** | First/oldest in Argentina | Pioneering in Australia |
| **Relationship Now** | Separate organizations, maintained friendship | Separate organizations, maintained friendship |

**Shared Themes**:
- Both childhood friends from same city (Salvador, Bahia)
- Trained together at same school (Mestre Alfredo's)
- Three-time Bahian champions together
- Arrived Argentina together (1987)
- Worked together 2-3 years
- Both became pioneers in new countries outside Brazil
- Both founded long-lasting institutions
- Maintained lifelong friendship despite separate paths

**Key Differences**:
- Val founded Filhos da Bahia in Brazil (1983) before Argentina trip
- Val had co-founders (brothers, Mestre Alfredo); Marcos solo
- Val established international network; Marcos focused regionally
- Val moved to Australia; Marcos stayed Argentina
- Val's organization named "Filhos da Bahia" (Sons of Bahia); Marcos chose "Oriaxé"

**Pattern**: Childhood friends, trained together, worked together briefly, then separated geographically to become pioneers in different countries, while maintaining friendship and mutual respect.

## Key Takeaway

**Grupo Oriaxé / Asociación Argentina de Capoeira represents a unique "childhood friends co-founding then splitting" pattern** - two best friends who trained together in Brazil, arrived together in a new country, worked together briefly, then separated geographically to become pioneers in different countries (Argentina and Australia) while maintaining lifelong friendship.

**What Makes This Group Unique**:

1. **Oldest Capoeira Institution in Argentina**: Pioneer status, teaching since 1987, official since 1995
2. **Childhood Friends Origin Story**: Marcos and Val trained together, championed together, migrated together
3. **Amicable Split Pattern**: Worked together 1987-1989, then Val left for Brazil/Australia, Marcos stayed
4. **Cultural Project Origin**: Invited for 5-day performance, stayed 38+ years
5. **Zero to Pioneer**: Arrived when capoeira completely unknown in Argentina
6. **Cultural Bridge**: Connected Brazilian culture to Argentine context via Egle Martin's Afro-Latin movement
7. **Long-Term Commitment**: 38 years teaching, 30 years as formal institution
8. **Sustained Impact**: 22+ batizados, multiple generations, 30th anniversary 2025

**Database Design Implications**:

1. **Childhood Friends Splitting**: Track when co-founders separate into independent organizations
2. **Pioneer Status by Region**: First capoeira in Argentina, South America (outside Brazil)
3. **Cultural Project Origins**: Schools founded via performance invitations
4. **Immigrant Founder Patterns**: Brazilian in Argentina (cultural/linguistic adaptation)
5. **Amicable vs. Hostile Splits**: Distinguish friendly separations from conflicts
6. **Maintained Friendships**: Track ongoing personal relationships despite organizational independence
7. **Teaching Before Formal Founding**: 1987 arrival vs. 1995 legal entity (years in limbo)
8. **Regional Concentration**: Multiple locations in one city vs. national/international expansion

**This case study demonstrates** how personal friendships, serendipitous cultural opportunities, and long-term commitment can establish capoeira in completely new regions. The childhood friends pattern shows how training relationships can lead to both collaboration and independent pioneering in different countries.
