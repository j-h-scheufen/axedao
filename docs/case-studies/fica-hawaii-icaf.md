# Case Study: Capoeira Angola Hawaii (FICA / ICAF)

**Date**: 2025-11-19
**ICAF Founded**: 1995-1996 (Washington DC)
**Hawaii Chapter Founded**: 2005 (Hilo, Big Island)
**ICAF Co-Founders**: Mestre Cobra Mansa, Mestre Jurandir Nascimento, Mestre Valmir Damasceno
**Hawaii Leader**: Trenel Joe (Joe Hoffman)
**Location**: Hilo and Pahoa, Big Island, Hawaii, USA
**Style**: Capoeira Angola (Traditional)
**Scale**: International - Largest Capoeira Angola organization worldwide

---

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

**Limited Scope**: This case study examines ONE chapter of the largest Capoeira Angola organization worldwide, focusing specifically on the Hawaii branch.

**Not Representative**: Observations here reflect only FICA Hawaii's specific structure and the broader ICAF/FICA organization. Other groups may have completely different organizational models.

**Preliminary Research**: All insights are preliminary and based on publicly available information. Community verification is essential before drawing any general conclusions about Capoeira organizational patterns.

**Historical Significance**: ICAF/FICA is critically important as:
1. The largest Capoeira Angola organization in the world
2. Founded by students who left GCAP in 1996 (a "blessed split")
3. Generation 3 from Mestre Pastinha (trained under Mestre Moraes)
4. Demonstrates successful international expansion model for Angola tradition

## Organizational Naming: FICA vs ICAF

**Same Organization, Two Names**:

**FICA**: **Fundação Internacional de Capoeira Angola**
- Portuguese acronym
- Commonly used in Brazil and Portuguese-speaking contexts

**ICAF**: **International Capoeira Angola Foundation**
- English acronym
- Commonly used in English-speaking countries (USA, etc.)

**This case study uses both names** as they appear interchangeably in sources, but they refer to the same organization.

## Founding History - The GCAP Split (1995-1996)

### The Three Co-Founders

**1. Mestre Cobra Mansa (Cobrinha)**
- **Birth Name**: Cinézio Feliciano Peçanha
- **Birthplace**: Valença, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
- **Born**: November 7, 1960
- **ICAF Role**: Co-founder, leads Washington DC headquarters
- **Current Location**: Washington DC, USA

**2. Mestre Jurandir Nascimento**
- **ICAF Role**: Co-founder, President of ICAF
- **Specialty**: Known for emphasis on music and cultural aspects
- **Teaching**: Based in US, travels internationally

**3. Mestre Valmir Damasceno**
- **ICAF Role**: Co-founder
- **Current Location**: Salvador, Bahia, Brazil
- **Teaching**: Leads FICA activities in Salvador

### Lineage Chain - Generation 3 from Pastinha

**Generation 0**: **Mestre Pastinha** (1889-1981)
- "Father of Capoeira Angola"
- Founded CECA (1941)

**Generation 1**: **Mestre João Grande + Mestre João Pequeno**
- Direct students of Pastinha
- Received diplomas from Pastinha (1968)

**Generation 2**: **Mestre Moraes**
- Student of João Grande and João Pequeno
- Founded GCAP (1980)
- Known as "O Ressuscitador da Tradição" (The Resuscitator of the Tradition)

**Generation 3**: **Cobra Mansa, Jurandir, Valmir**
- Students of Mestre Moraes at GCAP
- Founded ICAF/FICA (1995-1996)
- **THIS IS CRITICAL**: They maintain the direct lineage from Pastinha through their training

### The 1996 Split from GCAP

**Context**: In the mid-1990s, three of Mestre Moraes' students decided to establish their own organization.

**Nature of Split**: This appears to be a "blessed split" (similar to Grupo Capoeira Brasil from Senzala):
- Students had reached mestre level
- Ready to lead their own organization
- Maintained respect for Mestre Moraes and GCAP lineage
- No documented conflict or rupture

**Founding Year**: Sources vary between 1995 and 1996 - likely founded informally in 1995, formalized in 1996.

**Founding Model**: **Three co-founders, collaborative leadership**
- Similar to GCAP's four co-founders model
- Different from single-founder model (João Grande, Pastinha)
- Distributed leadership across three mestres

**Initial Focus**:
- Preserve and promote Capoeira Angola tradition
- International expansion from the start
- Emphasis on cultural and musical aspects
- Academic and research approach to tradition

## ICAF/FICA Organizational Characteristics

### Philosophy and Approach

**Cultural Preservation**:
- Maintain traditional Capoeira Angola as taught by Pastinha lineage
- Resist commercialization and dilution of tradition
- Emphasis on music, ritual, and cultural context

**Academic Approach**:
- Research and documentation of Capoeira Angola history
- Scholarly engagement with tradition
- Educational programs beyond just physical training

**International Vision**:
- Founded with explicit international mission
- Systematic expansion across continents
- Culturally sensitive local adaptation

### Teaching Methodology

**Core Components** (typical of ICAF/FICA):
1. **Physical Training**: Traditional Angola movements, groundwork, acrobatics
2. **Music**: All students learn instruments (berimbau, pandeiro, atabaque, agogô)
3. **Song**: Portuguese lyrics, traditional corridos and ladainhas
4. **Cultural Education**: History of Capoeira, African diaspora, Brazilian culture
5. **Philosophy**: Mandinga, malícia, jogo bonito concepts

**Emphasis on Music**:
- Mestre Jurandir particularly known for musical emphasis
- All students expected to participate in music circle
- Instruments and singing integral to advancement

### Leadership Development

**Title System** (observed in FICA Hawaii context):
- **Mestre**: Master level (founding mestres)
- **Contra-Mestre**: Not documented for ICAF
- **Trenel**: Advanced instructor level (Joe Hoffman's title)
- **Professor**: Instructor level (not confirmed for ICAF)
- **Monitor/Instrutor**: Assistant instructor levels (not confirmed)

**Note**: ICAF may have different title system than other organizations - requires community verification.

**All three ICAF founders trained extensively under Mestre Moraes at GCAP**:
- Learned traditional Angola from Generation 2 mestre (Moraes)

- Developed skills and philosophy before establishing ICAF

**Mestre Moraes' Legacy**:
- Academic approach (Master's degree, Doctorate, Grammy nomination)
- Cultural preservation emphasis
- International vision (convinced João Grande to return, trained international students)

### Nature of the Split

**Appears to be "Blessed Split"** (similar to Capoeira Brasil from Senzala):
1. Students reached mestre level under Moraes
2. Ready to lead and expand tradition independently
3. Maintained lineage connection and respect
4. No documented conflict or rupture
5. Both organizations continue to thrive

**Timing**: Mid-1990s
- GCAP founded 1980
- 15+ years of training under Moraes
- Mestres reached maturity and readiness to lead

**Geographic Factor**:
- Cobra Mansa moved to Washington DC (diaspora)
- Valmir remained in Salvador (Brazil)
- Created international structure from founding
