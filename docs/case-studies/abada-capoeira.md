# Case Study: ABADÁ-Capoeira

**Date**: 2025-11-19
**Status**: Initial Research
**Category**: Largest International Network, Regional/Contemporary Style

---

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

**Limited Scope**: This case study examines ONE group out of hundreds (possibly thousands) of Capoeira organizations worldwide.

**Not Representative**: Observations here reflect only this specific large international network. Smaller groups, regional organizations, Angola-focused groups, and independent mestres may have completely different structures and practices.

**Preliminary Research**: All insights are preliminary and based on publicly available information. Community verification is essential before drawing any general conclusions about Capoeira organizational patterns.

**No Universal Patterns**: What appears to be a "pattern" across 4 large networks may not apply to the broader Capoeira community. Further research across diverse group types is required.

---

## Overview

**ABADÁ-Capoeira** is widely considered the largest Capoeira organization in the world, founded in 1988 by brothers Mestre Camisa and Grão Mestre Camisa Roxa.

### Basic Information

- **Full Name**: Associação Brasileira de Apoio e Desenvolvimento da Arte-Capoeira (Brazilian Association for Support and Development of the Art of Capoeira)
- **Founded**: 1988 (Rio de Janeiro, Brazil)
- **Founders**: Mestre Camisa (José Tadeu Carneiro Cardoso) and his brother Grão Mestre Camisa Roxa
- **Style**: Contemporary (incorporates both Regional and Angola)
- **Lineage**: Direct students of Mestre Bimba (Regional lineage)
- **Scale**:
  - 41,000-80,000 members (estimates vary, likely grown over time)
  - 30-70+ countries
  - Every Brazilian state
  - Claims to be world's largest capoeira organization

**Notable**: Founded by brothers who both trained under Mestre Bimba.

### Digital Presence

**No central umbrella website** - highly decentralized web presence

**Local group examples**:
- Austria: http://www.abada-tirol.at/ (certificate expired)
- Germany: https://www.abadacapoeira.de/ (blocked)
- Washington DC: https://abadadc.org/
- Berlin: https://www.abada.net/
- Many others (each location maintains own site)

**Observation**: Most decentralized web presence of all major groups studied. Each location operates independently online with no central directory or location finder.

## Founding & Leadership

### The Brothers: Camisa and Camisa Roxa

**Background (Fazenda Estiva, Bahia)**:
- Grew up on a farm in northeast Brazil (Bahia state)
- **Camisa Roxa** (older brother) convinced their mother to let young José Tadeu attend Mestre Bimba's academy
- José Tadeu was 12 years old when he started training
- Both became students of **Mestre Bimba** in Salvador

**Lineage**: Mestre Bimba → Camisa + Camisa Roxa

### Mestre Camisa (José Tadeu Carneiro Cardoso)

- **Training**: Aluno Formado (graduated student) of Mestre Bimba
- **Early career**: Moved to Rio de Janeiro in early 1970s, began teaching
- **Founded ABADÁ**: 1988, Rio de Janeiro
- **Vision**: Create formal structure for teaching and preserving Capoeira with strong sense of family and community
- **Philosophy**: Developed own methodology following Bimba's innovative concepts
- **Unique rank**: Only person who holds **white cord (Diamond)** - highest rank in ABADÁ, no one else can achieve it
- **Current role**: President and founder

### Grão Mestre Camisa Roxa

- **Relationship**: Brother of Mestre Camisa
- **Training**: Also student of Mestre Bimba
- **Role**: Co-leader with Camisa
- **Title**: "Grão Mestre" (Grand Master) - unique title, different from regular Mestre

**Brother partnership pattern**: Like Muzenza (Mintirinha + Paulão brothers), ABADÁ has brother co-leadership

## Organizational Structure

### Observed Pattern

```
ABADÁ-Capoeira (No central corporate entity)
├─ Leadership
│   ├─ Mestre Camisa (President, Founder, White Cord - unique)
│   ├─ Grão Mestre Camisa Roxa (Co-leader, Brother)
│   └─ 10+ Mestres/Mestrandos (highest-ranked instructors)
│
├─ Brazil: Every state
│   └─ Independent local groups (legal entities)
│
└─ International: 30-70+ countries
    └─ Examples:
        ├─ ABADÁ-Capoeira Berlin (e.V. - German nonprofit)
        ├─ ABADÁ-Capoeira DC (Washington)
        ├─ ABADÁ-Capoeira Tirol (Austria)
        ├─ ABADÁ-Capoeira Marin (California)
        └─ Many more (each independent)
```

### Unique Organizational Characteristics

**1. No Central Corporation**:
- Unlike CDO (Associação with trademark) or Muzenza (central headquarters)
- ABADÁ appears to be a **network of independent entities** united by:
  - Shared name and philosophy
  - Common lineage (Mestre Bimba → Camisa/Roxa)
  - Standardized methodology
  - Recognition by Camisa/Roxa and senior Mestres

**2. Legal Independence**:
- Local groups are separate legal entities
- Example: Berlin is "e.V." (eingetragener Verein - registered German nonprofit)
- Each location handles its own legal/financial structure
- No apparent central licensing or certification system (like CDO)

**3. Cultural Unity, Legal Autonomy**:
- Groups identify as "Teil von Abadá-Capoeira" (part of ABADÁ-Capoeira)
- Follow standardized methodology and graduation system
- But legally and financially independent

**Questions**:
- Is there a formal affiliation process?
- Can Camisa/Roxa revoke a group's use of the ABADÁ name?
- Is there a trademark, or just cultural affiliation?
- How are new groups approved/recognized?

## Key Features

### 1. Brother Co-Leadership

**Unique partnership**:
- Biological brothers
- Both students of Mestre Bimba
- Co-founded organization together
- Different titles: Mestre (Camisa) vs. Grão Mestre (Camisa Roxa)
- Both listed as primary leaders

**Genealogy implications**:
- Family relationship (brothers)
- Teacher-student relationship (both studied under Bimba)
- Peer relationship (co-founders)
- Multiple overlapping relationship types

### 2. Unique Rank: White Cord (Diamond)

**Camisa's exclusive rank**:
- Only Mestre Camisa holds white cord
- No one else in ABADÁ can achieve this rank
- Creates unique hierarchy with founder at apex
- Different from other groups where multiple people can become Mestre

**Comparison**:
- CDO: Multiple mestres (Suassuna, Brasília, regional mestres)
- Muzenza: Multiple mestres (Burguês, 706 instructors including mestres)
- Axé: Multiple mestres (Barrão, local leaders)
- ABADÁ: ONE white cord (Camisa), multiple regular mestres below

**Data model implication**: Some groups have unique founder ranks separate from regular hierarchy

### 3. Standardized Graduation System

**Hierarchical colored cord system**:
- Progresses from beginner to Mestre
- Standardized across all ABADÁ groups worldwide
- Specialized systems:
  - Youth program (ages 3-16)
  - Baobá system (seniors/elders)
- Ensures consistency across 80,000 members

**Enables massive scale**: Like Muzenza's 22 sequences, standardization allows replication

### 4. Bimba Lineage Connection

**Direct lineage**: Mestre Bimba → Camisa + Camisa Roxa → 80,000 students

**Genealogy significance**:
- Part of the broader Mestre Bimba student network
- Connects to other Bimba students:
  - Mestre Acordeon
  - Mestre Nenel (Bimba's son)
  - Grupo Capoeira Brasil founders (Peixinho → Boneco, etc.)
- Regional/Contemporary lineage (not Angola)

### 5. Philosophy: Regional + Angola Integration

**Methodology**:
- Follows Bimba's innovative concepts (Regional foundation)
- Seeks to incorporate Angola practices
- Contemporary approach blending both styles
- Emphasis on technical mastery AND cultural preservation

**Positioning**: Similar to CDO and Muzenza - contemporary synthesis rather than purist

### 6. Decentralized Web Presence

**No central website**:
- Each local group maintains own site
- No central directory or location finder
- Very different from:
  - CDO's organized continent/country directory
  - Axé's location finder
  - Even Muzenza's unstructured list

**Implication**: Most independent local web operations of any large group studied

## Observed Patterns

### 1. Brother Partnership Model

**Family + training bonds**:
- Camisa Roxa brought young Camisa to Bimba
- Both trained together under Bimba
- Co-founded ABADÁ together
- Co-lead organization together

**Pattern observed**:
- Muzenza: Mintirinha (brother) taught Paulão (brother/founder)
- ABADÁ: Camisa Roxa + Camisa (brothers, co-founders)
- Axé: Barrão + Barrãozinho (father-son)

**Genealogy insight**: Family relationships are significant in Capoeira lineage, not just teacher-student

### 2. Largest Organization by Membership

**Scale comparison**:
| Organization | Members | Countries | Notes |
|--------------|---------|-----------|-------|
| ABADÁ | 41,000-80,000 | 30-70+ | Largest by membership |
| Muzenza | ~25,000 (2000) | 61 | 706 registered instructors |
| CDO | Unknown | Multiple continents | Older (1967) |
| Axé | Unknown | Unknown | Large but smaller than ABADÁ |

**How achieved**:
- Standardized methodology (easy to replicate)
- Strong brand identity (Bimba lineage)
- Youth programs (ages 3-16 - early recruitment)
- Senior programs (lifelong participation)
- 37 years of growth (1988-2025)

### 3. Decentralized Legal Structure

**Network model**:
- No central corporation
- Each location is independent legal entity
- Cultural and methodological unity
- Legal and financial autonomy

**Comparison**:
- **CDO**: Central Associação, trademark, certification system (centralized)
- **Axé**: Direct branches of central org (centralized)
- **Muzenza**: Registration system, headquarters in Curitiba (semi-centralized)
- **ABADÁ**: Network of independents (decentralized)

**Observed variation among these 4 large networks** (not generalizable):
```
More Centralized ←------------------→ More Decentralized
(CDO/Axé)           (Muzenza)              (ABADÁ)
```

**Note**: This is only observed among 4 large international networks. Many other organizational models likely exist in the broader Capoeira community.

### 4. Unique Founder Rank

**White Cord exclusive to Camisa**:
- Creates permanent distinction between founder and all others
- No succession issue (no one can replace him as White Cord)
- Regular Mestres exist below him in hierarchy

**Contrast with**:
- CDO: Co-founders both Mestres
- Muzenza: Burguês is president, but others can be Mestre
- Axé: Barrão is Mestre, but so are others

**Insight**: Some founders create unique ranks for themselves, others share highest rank with students

### 5. Family-Founded Organizations

**Brother co-founding pattern emerging**:
- ABADÁ: Camisa + Camisa Roxa (brothers)
- Muzenza: Paulão student of Mintirinha (his brother)
- Axé: Barrão + Barrãozinho (father-son succession)

**Data model need**: Track family relationships alongside training relationships

| Aspect | ABADÁ | Muzenza | CDO | Axé |
|--------|-------|---------|-----|-----|
| **Founded** | 1988 | 1972 → 1978 | 1967 | 1982 |
| **Founders** | Brothers (Camisa + Camisa Roxa) | Paulão → Burguês | Suassuna + Brasília | Barrão |

4. **Web presence correlates with centralization** (CDO centralized → organized site; ABADÁ decentralized → no central site)

## Key Takeaway

ABADÁ represents a **decentralized network model** - the opposite end of the spectrum from CDO's centralized certification system. This shows that large-scale Capoeira organizations can succeed with very different governance structures:

- **CDO**: Centralized (trademark, licensing, certification, fees)
- **ABADÁ**: Decentralized (independent legal entities, cultural affiliation, no apparent central fees)

Both models work, serving different cultural values and organizational philosophies.
