## Biography / Biografia

### English

Manoel Carregador was a capoeirista present at the famous Roça do Lobo roda in Salvador in 1953. His apelido "Carregador" (porter/carrier) suggests he worked as a dock worker or porter, a common occupation among Salvador's capoeiristas during this era.

The only documented reference to Manoel Carregador comes from Mestre João Grande's testimony about his first encounter with capoeira. In 1953, João Grande—then a young man of 20 who had just arrived in Salvador—came upon a roda "underneath the mango tree" near the bridge that linked Tororó to Garcia, in the impoverished neighborhood known as Roça do Lobo. There he found a gathering of notable capoeiristas: João Pequeno, Barbosa, Gordo (Menino Gordo), Cobrinha Verde, Tiburcinho, and Manoel Carregador.

This roda was a pivotal moment in capoeira history. João Grande asked what he was witnessing and was told "This is capoeira!" He asked to learn, and the capoeiristas directed him to João Pequeno, who in turn introduced him to Mestre Pastinha—thus beginning one of the most important lineages in capoeira Angola.

Like his contemporary Barbosa (also a carregador), Manoel Carregador represents the working-class men who preserved and transmitted capoeira through informal street rodas during the mid-20th century. The presence of multiple carregadores at this roda reflects the deep connection between capoeira and Salvador's port and market workers.

Ele pediu para aprender, e os capoeiristas o direcionaram a João Pequeno, que por sua vez o apresentou a Mestre Pastinha—iniciando assim uma das linhagens mais importantes da capoeira Angola.

Assim como seu contemporâneo Barbosa (também um carregador), Manoel Carregador representa os homens da classe trabalhadora que preservaram e transmitiram a capoeira através de rodas de rua informais durante meados do século XX. A presença de múltiplos carregadores nessa roda reflete a profunda conexão entre a capoeira e os trabalhadores do porto e dos mercados de Salvador.

Roça do Lobo was an impoverished neighborhood (invasão) near the bridge connecting Tororó to Garcia in Salvador. The roda took place under a mango tree. This area is approximately where the Instituto Pedro Melo stands today on Av. Centenário.
