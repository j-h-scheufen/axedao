## Biography / Biografia

### English

Mestre Vinte e Nove was a direct disciple of Mestre Bimba, the founder of Capoeira Regional. His apelido—meaning "Twenty-Nine" in Portuguese—likely derives from the jogo do bicho, the popular Brazilian numbers game where 29 represents the Camelo (Camel), a symbol of endurance and resilience. Such number-based nicknames were common among capoeiristas of his era in Salvador, with his contemporary Vermelho 27 carrying a similar numerical apelido.

The historical record places Vinte e Nove among the most prominent Capoeira Regional mestres of his generation. In February 1982, O Globo newspaper published an article titled "Capoeira - Uma grande polêmica nas rodas baianas" (Capoeira - A great controversy in the Bahia rodas), documenting the state of capoeira in Salvador. The article surveyed the capoeira landscape by lineage, identifying the leading figures in each tradition. For the "regional" branch, it noted that "almost all were direct disciples of the legendary Bimba", and specifically named Vinte e Nove among the most distinguished mestres. He appears in this documentation alongside Itapuã, Brás Amansa Brabo, Vermelho 27, Medicina, Banduê, Capazans, Ezequiel, and Hélio Xaréu.

This 1982 press coverage confirms that Vinte e Nove had achieved recognition as a mestre and was actively teaching and participating in the Salvador capoeira community at that time. His inclusion in this select list of "prominent" regional mestres indicates he was considered among the leading practitioners carrying forward Bimba's legacy.

Beyond this documentation, little else has been preserved about his life. His full name, precise birth and death dates, the details of his training timeline with Bimba, and information about his own students remain undocumented in available sources. Like several of his contemporaries named in the 1982 article, Vinte e Nove represents a mestre whose individual story has been overshadowed by the more extensively documented figures of the Regional lineage. His presence in this significant press coverage, however, confirms his historical importance as one of the direct links in the chain connecting Mestre Bimba to subsequent generations of Capoeira Regional.

Para o ramo "regional", observou que "quase todos eram discípulos diretos do lendário Bimba", e nomeou especificamente Vinte e Nove entre os mestres mais distintos. Ele aparece nesta documentação ao lado de Itapuã, Brás Amansa Brabo, Vermelho 27, Medicina, Banduê, Capazans, Ezequiel e Hélio Xaréu.

Esta cobertura de imprensa de 1982 confirma que Vinte e Nove havia alcançado reconhecimento como mestre e estava ativamente ensinando e participando da comunidade de capoeira de Salvador naquela época. Sua inclusão nesta lista seleta de mestres regional "proeminentes" indica que ele era considerado entre os principais praticantes que levavam adiante o legado de Bimba.

Além desta documentação, pouco mais foi preservado sobre sua vida. Seu nome completo, datas precisas de nascimento e morte, os detalhes de sua linha do tempo de treinamento com Bimba, e informações sobre seus próprios alunos permanecem não documentados nas fontes disponíveis. Como vários de seus contemporâneos nomeados no artigo de 1982, Vinte e Nove representa um mestre cuja história individual foi ofuscada por figuras mais extensivamente documentadas da linhagem Regional. Sua presença nesta significativa cobertura de imprensa, no entanto, confirma sua importância histórica como um dos elos diretos na corrente conectando Mestre Bimba às gerações subsequentes de Capoeira Regional.

### Apelido Meaning
"Vinte e Nove" (Twenty-Nine) likely derives from the jogo do bicho, the popular Brazilian numbers game. In the jogo do bicho, 29 belongs to Group 8, represented by the Camelo (Camel), symbolizing endurance and resilience. Number-based apelidos were common among Salvador capoeiristas of this era—his contemporary Vermelho 27 carried a similar numerical nickname.
