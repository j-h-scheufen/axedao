## Biography / Biografia

### English

Tatu Bola was a capoeirista active in Salvador, Bahia, during the 1940s and 1950s. He is documented in the oral testimony of Mestre Roque, who recalled socializing with Tatu Bola and other famous capoeiristas of the era at the Mercado Modelo through his father Chico Preto's connections in the capoeira community.

The Mercado Modelo was a central hub of Bahian culture and capoeira during the mid-20th century. The Rampa do Mercado (Market Ramp) was a renowned gathering place for capoeiristas, and the market's vendors and workers were connected to the vibrant community of practitioners who worked and played there. Mestre Waldemar had begun selling his hand-painted berimbaus at the nearby Água de Meninos fair in 1942, and mestres like Bugalho were fixtures at the market ramp.

Roque, who was ten years old when he began learning capoeira in 1948, remembered the figures he encountered through his father: Gajé, Índio, Tatu Bola, Bom Cabrito, Boca de Fumaça, Traíra, and Canjiquinha. These were the famous capoeiristas of the transition era—when capoeira had been legalized in 1937 after decades of prohibition, and the art was beginning to move from the streets into academies.

No primary source documentation has been found regarding Tatu Bola's full name, formal title, teachers, students, or specific activities. His apelido ("tatu-bola" refers to the three-banded armadillo, known for curling into a protective ball) suggests a connection to the northeastern Brazilian fauna and may reflect his playing style or physical characteristics.

## Apelido Meaning / Significado do Apelido

"Tatu-bola" (literally "ball armadillo") is the Brazilian Portuguese name for the three-banded armadillo (*Tolypeutes tricinctus*), endemic to northeastern Brazil. The animal is known for its unique defense mechanism of rolling into a near-perfect ball when threatened. This apelido may reflect:
- A defensive playing style
- Physical characteristics (compact build)
- A nickname given at batizado
- Connection to northeastern Brazil

## Historical Context / Contexto Histórico

### The Mercado Modelo Scene (1940s-1950s)

The Mercado Modelo was central to Salvador's capoeira community in the mid-20th century:
- Opened in 1912 as a popular market
- The Rampa do Mercado (Market Ramp) was a famous capoeira location
- Mestre Waldemar began selling berimbaus at Água de Meninos fair in 1942
- Mestre Bugalho was a fixture at the market ramp
- Mestre Camafeu de Oxóssi obtained his first stall there in 1945
- The original market was destroyed by fire on August 1, 1969

### The Transition Era (1930s-1950s)

Tatu Bola was active during a pivotal period:
- Capoeira legalized in 1937 after decades of prohibition
- Mestre Bimba founded first official academy (1932)
- Mestre Pastinha formalized Capoeira Angola (1940s)
- Art transitioning from streets to academies
