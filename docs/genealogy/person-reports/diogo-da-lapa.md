## Biography / Biografia

### English

Diogo da Lapa, also known as Fernão Diogo, was a capoeirista active in Rio de Janeiro during the tumultuous final years of the Brazilian Empire. His full name was Diogo Francisco de Oliveira, and he was associated with the capoeira maltas of the Largo da Lapa neighborhood, one of the traditional strongholds of the Nagoas faction.

In the aftermath of the abolition of slavery on May 13, 1888, the capoeira maltas of Rio de Janeiro became deeply involved in the political conflicts between monarchists and republicans. The Conservative Party, which had overseen abolition through the Lei Áurea (Golden Law), sought to harness the loyalty of freed slaves and capoeiristas to defend the monarchy. From this alliance emerged the Guarda Negra da Redentora (Black Guard of the Redemptress), a paramilitary organization composed of former slaves and capoeiristas who aimed to protect Princess Isabel and the imperial regime against the rising Republican Party.

The Guarda Negra was formally organized by the abolitionist journalist José do Patrocínio on July 9, 1888, at the editorial office of the newspaper *Cidade do Rio*. Its first commander was Clarindo de Almeida. The organization recruited heavily from the Nagoas maltas—the capoeira gangs that controlled the southern and western parishes of Rio, including Lapa, Santana, Glória, and São José. These groups had long-standing ties to the Conservative Party and saw the defense of the monarchy as a defense of their newly won freedom.

Diogo da Lapa participated in the Guarda Negra operating in the Largo da Lapa area, as well as with a group of ten capoeiras in the Santana neighborhood. The Lapa and Santana regions were traditional Nagoas territory. The Largo da Lapa was specifically the domain of the Espada malta, one of the most feared capoeira gangs in the city. The maltas of this area provided many of the shock troops for the Guarda Negra's violent confrontations with Republican rallies—most notably the assault on the French Gymnastic Society on December 30, 1888, where members of the Guard attacked republicans listening to the orator Silva Jardim.

The Proclamation of the Republic on November 15, 1889, marked the beginning of a violent campaign against capoeiristas. The new police chief, João Batista Sampaio Ferraz—known as "Cavanhaque de Aço" (Steel Goatee)—immediately began mass arrests and deportations. Sampaio Ferraz had vowed to exterminate capoeiras after witnessing a violent dispute between two maltas that left an old man with a sliced back, a boy with a cut lip, and a pregnant woman with deep wounds to her belly.

In a single week from December 12-18, 1889, 111 capoeiras were arrested. Diogo da Lapa was among those detained in mid-December 1889, arrested specifically for his participation in the Guarda Negra.

His fate after arrest is unknown. Many of the captured capoeiras were deported to the remote island prison of Fernando de Noronha, where they were subjected to forced labor. On January 3, 1890, a war steamer carrying 154 capoeiras set sail for the island. According to Sampaio Ferraz's biographer, within the first forty days of the Republican regime, an estimated 1,300 capoeiras had been exiled there. In 1890, the new Republic formally criminalized capoeira through Article 402 of the Republican Penal Code.

## Historical Context / Contexto Histórico

### The Capoeira Maltas / As Maltas de Capoeira

During the Second Empire (1850-1889), Rio de Janeiro's capoeiristas were organized into territorial gangs called *maltas*. These maltas were divided into two rival federations:

- **Nagoas**: Composed primarily of enslaved Africans, freed Blacks, and their descendants. They controlled the rural and southern parishes (Glória, Lapa, Santana, São José, Santa Luzia). Allied with the Conservative Party. Their color was white.
- **Guaiamús**: Composed of mixed-race individuals, poor whites, and Portuguese immigrants. They controlled the commercial center and port zones. Allied with the Liberal Party.

### The Espada Malta / A Malta Espada

The Largo da Lapa was controlled by the Espada ("Sword") malta, one of the Nagoas gangs. The battle cry "Entra Espada!" (Enter Espada!) was a challenge to rival gangs. The Espada was known for its violent conflicts with the Marinha gang from the port area (Guaiamú territory).

### The Guarda Negra / A Guarda Negra

After abolition, the Guarda Negra united capoeiristas from the Nagoas maltas to defend the monarchy. Key dates:

- **May 13, 1888**: Lei Áurea abolishes slavery
- **July 9, 1888**: Guarda Negra formally organized by José do Patrocínio
- **December 30, 1888**: Major attack on Republican rally at French Gymnastic Society
- **November 15, 1889**: Proclamation of the Republic
- **December 12-18, 1889**: 111 capoeiras arrested in one week (Diogo among them)
- **January 3, 1890**: First deportation ship to Fernando de Noronha (154 capoeiras)
- **1890**: Capoeira criminalized by Republican Penal Code, Article 402
