## Biography / Biografia

### English

Marco Buscapé was a capoeirista from Salvador, Bahia, active in the mid-20th century. He was the son and student of Zé Bedeu, who passed on the art of capoeira to him.

Marco Buscapé was the cousin of Mestre Roque (Roque Mendes dos Santos, born 1938 in Cachoeira), who would become one of the four founding lineages of contemporary capoeira in Rio de Janeiro. Through his father Zé Bedeu—who was the brother of Chico Preto (Liberato Francisco Xavier)—Marco was part of a family of capoeiristas that included his uncle and cousin.

The family was rooted in the Nazaré neighborhood of Salvador, where Chico Preto raised his family. Both Zé Bedeu and Chico Preto were capoeiristas during a pivotal era—the period surrounding the legalization of capoeira in 1937, when the art transitioned from illegal street practice to recognized cultural expression.

**Important distinction:** Marco Buscapé (son of Zé Bedeu) is a distinct person from Mário Buscapé (1934-2021), the famous mestre from São Francisco do Conde who founded the Capoeiras do Bonfim group in Rio de Janeiro. Mário Buscapé's father was José Bidel dos Santos (Mestre Bidel), not Zé Bedeu. These represent two separate family lineages in capoeira history.

### Português

Marco Buscapé foi um capoeirista de Salvador, Bahia, ativo em meados do século XX. Era filho e aluno de Zé Bedeu, que lhe transmitiu a arte da capoeira.

Marco Buscapé era primo de Mestre Roque (Roque Mendes dos Santos, nascido em 1938 em Cachoeira), que se tornaria uma das quatro linhagens fundadoras da capoeira contemporânea no Rio de Janeiro. Através de seu pai Zé Bedeu—que era irmão de Chico Preto (Liberato Francisco Xavier)—Marco fazia parte de uma família de capoeiristas que incluía seu tio e primo.

A família tinha raízes no bairro de Nazaré em Salvador, onde Chico Preto criou sua família. Tanto Zé Bedeu quanto Chico Preto eram capoeiristas durante uma era crucial—o período em torno da legalização da capoeira em 1937, quando a arte passou de prática de rua ilegal para expressão cultural reconhecida.

**Distinção importante:** Marco Buscapé (filho de Zé Bedeu) é uma pessoa distinta de Mário Buscapé (1934-2021), o famoso mestre de São Francisco do Conde que fundou o grupo Capoeiras do Bonfim no Rio de Janeiro. O pai de Mário Buscapé era José Bidel dos Santos (Mestre Bidel), não Zé Bedeu. Estas representam duas linhagens familiares separadas na história da capoeira.

### Family Capoeira Network
Marco Buscapé was part of a family where capoeira was passed down through generations. This network included:

- **Zé Bedeu** - Father; capoeirista; Marco's primary teacher
- **Chico Preto** - Uncle (Zé Bedeu's brother); worked at Mercado Modelo, a hub for capoeira in Salvador
- **Mestre Roque** - Cousin; became one of four founding lineages of Rio de Janeiro capoeira

Through his uncle Chico Preto who worked at Mercado Modelo, Marco Buscapé was connected (at least indirectly) to the famous gathering place for capoeiristas in Salvador. Notable figures at the market included:

- Bom Cabrito
- Boca de Fumaça

## Notes & Uncertainties / Notas e Incertezas

### Limited Documentation
Marco Buscapé is only documented through a single source: the biography of his cousin Mestre Roque at capoeuropa.org (Escola de Capoeira Angola Nzinga). No independent biographical sources have been found. The confidence level for this person is "likely" rather than "verified".

The source states: "Zé Bedeu (pai e mestre de Marco Buscapé)" - literally "Zé Bedeu (father and master of Marco Buscapé)".

### Name Clarification
The use of "mestre" in "pai e mestre" likely uses the word in the sense of "teacher" rather than as a formal capoeira title for Zé Bedeu. There is no documentation of Zé Bedeu holding a formal title.
