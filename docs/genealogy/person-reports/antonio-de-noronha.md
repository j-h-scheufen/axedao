## Biography / Biografia

### English

Mestre Antônio de Noronha is a shadowy but critical figure in capoeira Angola history. He exists primarily through oral tradition and lineage claims, with almost no biographical details surviving.

What we know with reasonable confidence:

Around 1910-1912, a young Aberrê (Antônio Raimundo Argolo, born 1895) learned capoeira in Salvador. The reliable source velhosmestres.com states he "learned capoeira with M Pastinha and/or with M Antônio de Noronha" during this period. The Grupo Esquiva de Rua website lists Aberrê's "Origem: Mestre Antonio de Noronha"—identifying him as Aberrê's primary teacher.

The strongest evidence comes from direct testimony. According to the Esquiva website, "Mestre Aberrê is the second mestre in my lineage, above him comes Mestre Antônio de Noronha—this affirmation came from Mestre Caiçara and Mestre Silvestre." Mestre Silvestre (Silvestre Vitório Ferreira, "Ferrerinha") was a student of both Mestre Waldemar and Mestre Caiçara, and his testimony carries weight. Grupo Gingarte also follows this lineage, confirming the chain: Antônio de Noronha → Aberrê → Canjiquinha.

**Who was he?** If he was teaching in 1910-1912, and assuming he was at least 30-40 years old when teaching (the typical age for a recognized mestre in that era), he was likely born between 1870 and 1880. This places him in the generation that learned capoeira when it was first criminalized in Brazil (1890) and practiced it in hiding or under police persecution.

He likely taught in the Matatu neighborhood, as this is where Aberrê trained. The "Baixada do Matatu Preto" became an important capoeira training ground in the early 20th century.

## Why This Person Matters / Por Que Esta Pessoa Importa

### English
Antônio de Noronha represents an invisible but essential link in capoeira genealogy. Without him, the lineage chain Aberrê → Canjiquinha → modern groups would have no "upstream" anchor. He is evidence that the oral tradition of capoeira lineage extends back further than the well-documented mestres like Pastinha and Bimba.

His near-disappearance from the historical record illustrates how capoeira history has been selectively preserved—favoring figures who wrote books, founded academies, or became famous through other means. The unnamed teachers who transmitted capoeira in the criminalized era are largely forgotten.

### Português

Seu quase desaparecimento do registro histórico ilustra como a história da capoeira foi seletivamente preservada—favorecendo figuras que escreveram livros, fundaram academias, ou se tornaram famosos por outros meios. Os professores sem nome que transmitiram a capoeira na era criminalizada são em grande parte esquecidos.
