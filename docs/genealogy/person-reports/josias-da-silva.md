## Biography / Biografia

### English

Mestre Josias da Silva was a pioneering figure in the development of capoeira in the Baixada Fluminense region of Rio de Janeiro. He learned capoeira from Mestre Paulo Gomes (1941-1998), who had moved to São João de Meriti in the 1960s after training under Mestre Artur Emídio in Rio de Janeiro. This placed Josias in a distinguished lineage tracing back through generations of Bahian masters: Neném → Paizinho (Teodoro Ramos) → Artur Emídio → Paulo Gomes → Josias da Silva.

Josias established the Associação de Capoeira Josias da Silva, which operated in the municipalities of Nova Iguaçu and Duque de Caxias in the Baixada Fluminense. Along with Mestre Barbosa, he was one of the pioneer mestres who ran the first formal capoeira academies in Duque de Caxias.

Sources describe him as a "difusor de um estilo de capoeira esportiva" (promoter of a sports capoeira style), emphasizing the athletic and competitive aspects of capoeira. This approach contrasted with the more traditional street capoeira that would later emerge from the Roda Livre de Caxias.

In 1973, a young Cinézio Feliciano Peçanha—who would become the internationally renowned Mestre Cobra Mansa, co-founder of GCAP and founder of FICA—began learning capoeira with Josias and Raimundo in Duque de Caxias. The following year, Cobra Mansa would encounter Mestre Moraes at the Roda do Central do Brasil and follow a path into Capoeira Angola, but his foundation began in the academies of these Baixada Fluminense pioneers.

The young capoeiristas training at the academies of Josias and Barbosa would eventually break with the academy model during the height of the military dictatorship. On June 13, 1973, during the Festa da Igreja de Santo Antônio, they founded the Roda Livre de Caxias—a street roda that continues to this day. This act was considered "sacrilege" by the pioneer mestres who valued the structured academy environment, but it created a new tradition that would produce its own generation of mestres.

Beyond Cobra Mansa, Josias trained numerous other capoeiristas who would become mestres, including Canela, Raimundo, Butt, Crispin, Irani, and Daniel Canedo. Mestre Daniel Canedo, born in 1961 in Duque de Caxias, trained at Josias's association from the mid-1970s until the late 1980s before establishing capoeira in Canavieiras, Bahia.

Os jovens capoeiristas que treinavam nas academias de Josias e Barbosa eventualmente romperiam com o modelo de academia durante o auge da ditadura militar. Esse ato foi considerado "sacrilégio" pelos mestres pioneiros que valorizavam o ambiente estruturado da academia, mas criou uma nova tradição que produziria sua própria geração de mestres.

Mestre Daniel Canedo, nascido em 1961 em Duque de Caxias, treinou na associação de Josias de meados dos anos 1970 até o final dos anos 1980, antes de estabelecer a capoeira em Canavieiras, Bahia.

## Researcher Notes / Notas do Pesquisador

### English

Birth year is estimated as mid-1940s (using decade precision). Josias was teaching Cobra Mansa in 1973 when Cobra Mansa was 13, and had already established an academy and trained under Paulo Gomes in the 1960s. This suggests he was at least in his late 20s or 30s by 1973.

The ACAZ website (acaz.site/historicoacaz) states that "Josias began practicing capoeira in 1973 with Raimundo" but this conflicts with him being Paulo Gomes's student in the 1960s. The 1973 date more likely refers to when Josias and Raimundo began teaching together in Duque de Caxias, not when they started their capoeira training.

No information on death date has been found.

Josias estava ensinando Cobra Mansa em 1973 quando Cobra Mansa tinha 13 anos, e já havia estabelecido uma academia e treinado com Paulo Gomes nos anos 1960. Isso sugere que ele tinha pelo menos seus 20 ou 30 anos em 1973.

A data de 1973 provavelmente se refere a quando Josias e Raimundo começaram a ensinar juntos em Duque de Caxias, não quando começaram seu treinamento de capoeira.
