## Biography | Biografia

### EN

Paula Cristina da Silva Barreto began training capoeira near the start of the 1980s at GCAP (Grupo de Capoeira Angola Pelourinho) in Salvador, under the guidance of Mestres Moraes, João Grande, and Cobra Mansa. Over fifteen years, she immersed herself in the GCAP community, participating since its foundation and developing into one of its prominent practitioners. In 1990, she achieved the rank of contra-mestra—recognition of her deep commitment to the art.

During this period, Paulinha pursued parallel paths in capoeira and academia. She completed her Master's degree in Sociology at the Federal University of Bahia (UFBA) in 1993, joining the faculty as a professor in 1994. Her doctoral studies took her to São Paulo, where she earned her PhD in Sociology from the University of São Paulo (USP) in 2003—a thesis exploring the intersections of race, racism, and social resistance.

In 1995, while pursuing her doctorate, Paulinha co-founded the Instituto Nzinga de Estudos da Capoeira Angola e Tradições Educativas Banto no Brasil alongside Mestra Janja (Rosângela Costa Araújo) and Mestre Poloca (Paulo Barreto). All three were fellow disciples from GCAP who shared a vision of capoeira as an instrument of anti-racist and anti-sexist struggle. The group was named after Queen Nzinga Mbandi Ngola (1581-1663), the Angolan warrior queen who led resistance against Portuguese colonization.

After moving to São Paulo in 1998 to coordinate INCAB during her doctoral work and completing her doctorate in 2002, Paulinha returned to Salvador, where she leads the Salvador nucleus of Grupo Nzinga alongside her academic work.

Her academic career is distinguished. She became Professora Titular (Full Professor) in the Department of Sociology at UFBA, coordinating the Laboratory/Program "A Cor da Bahia" (The Color of Bahia: Research and Training Program on Racial Relations, Culture and Black Identity in Bahia). She has served as Director of CEAO (Centro de Estudos Afro-Orientais). Her scholarship focuses on the sociology of racial relations, intersectionality, decoloniality, and anti-racism. She completed postdoctoral work at Vanderbilt University (2007-2008) and University of Brasília (2017-2018).

In 2017, Paulinha was invited to the Women's Conference 20th Anniversary in Washington, D.C., sponsored by FICA, celebrating women's contributions to Capoeira Angola.

Today, Grupo Nzinga operates in five Brazilian cities and twelve international cities including Maputo, London, Tokyo, Kyoto, and Atlanta.

Em 1995, enquanto cursava seu doutorado, Paulinha co-fundou o Instituto Nzinga de Estudos da Capoeira Angola e Tradições Educativas Banto no Brasil ao lado de Mestra Janja (Rosângela Costa Araújo) e Mestre Poloca (Paulo Barreto). O grupo foi nomeado em homenagem à Rainha Nzinga Mbandi Ngola (1581-1663), a rainha guerreira angolana que liderou resistência contra a colonização portuguesa.

Após mudar-se para São Paulo em 1998 para coordenar o INCAB durante seu trabalho de doutorado e completar seu doutorado em 2002, Paulinha retornou a Salvador, onde lidera o núcleo de Salvador do Grupo Nzinga junto com seu trabalho acadêmico.

Sua carreira acadêmica é distinta. Tornou-se Professora Titular no Departamento de Sociologia da UFBA, coordenando o Laboratório/Programa "A Cor da Bahia" (Programa de Pesquisa e Formação sobre Relações Raciais, Cultura e Identidade Negra na Bahia). Completou pós-doutorado na Vanderbilt University (2007-2008) e Universidade de Brasília (2017-2018).

Em 2017, Paulinha foi convidada para a Women's Conference 20th Anniversary em Washington, D.C., patrocinada pela FICA, celebrando as contribuições das mulheres para a Capoeira Angola.

Hoje, o Grupo Nzinga opera em cinco cidades brasileiras e doze cidades internacionais incluindo Maputo, Londres, Tóquio, Kyoto e Atlanta.
