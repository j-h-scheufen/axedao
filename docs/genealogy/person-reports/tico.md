## Biography / Biografia

### English

Julio dos Santos Menezes, known as Tico, was a dock worker and capoeirista from Santo Amaro in the Recôncavo Baiano region who became part of the legendary community of capoeiristas at the Cais Dourado (Golden Wharf) in Salvador during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Tico worked as a carregador (porter) and frequented the Freguesia do Pilar, the parish that encompassed the waterfront district where dock workers, stevedores, and saveiristas congregated. The Cais Dourado was notorious as a stronghold of capoeira—a universe of maritime laborers who breathed the culture of sailors in their daily lives and practiced their art amid the bustle of commerce and cargo.

The historical record captures Tico through a violent incident: he was arrested following an armed confrontation in which he wielded a faca de sapateiro (shoemaker's knife) against "Martins' group." The fight left him with cuts to his arms, face, and hands. This documentation, likely from police records, is typical of how Layer Zero capoeiristas entered the historical record—not through celebrations of their art, but through encounters with authorities during the era of criminalization.

Tico's contemporaries at the Cais Dourado included some of the most storied names in early capoeira: the legendary Besouro Mangangá who arrived from Santo Amaro via saveiro, Pedro Porreta the fish seller who dominated the Baixa dos Sapateiros, Caboclinho the saveirista, and Antônio Corró the cart driver who would later teach Mestre Paraná. Together they formed a community of working-class practitioners who kept capoeira alive through the years of prohibition, meeting at the waterfront, at the Mercado Santa Bárbara, and during festivals like the Santa Luzia celebration documented by Mestre Noronha in 1922.

Like many of his generation, Tico's fate after the early 20th century is unknown. He remains one of the anonymous ancestors—African and Afro-Brazilian workers who preserved and transmitted the art during its most dangerous years.

O Cais Dourado era notório como um reduto da capoeira—um universo de trabalhadores marítimos que respiravam a cultura dos marinheiros em seu cotidiano e praticavam sua arte em meio à agitação do comércio e da carga.

O registro histórico captura Tico através de um incidente violento: ele foi preso após um confronto armado no qual empunhou uma faca de sapateiro contra o "grupo de Martins". A briga deixou-o com cortes nos braços, rosto e mãos. Esta documentação, provavelmente de registros policiais, é típica de como os capoeiristas da Camada Zero entraram no registro histórico—não através de celebrações de sua arte, mas através de encontros com as autoridades durante a era de criminalização.

Os contemporâneos de Tico no Cais Dourado incluíam alguns dos nomes mais célebres da capoeira antiga: o lendário Besouro Mangangá que chegava de Santo Amaro via saveiro, Pedro Porreta o vendedor de peixe que dominava a Baixa dos Sapateiros, Caboclinho o saveirista, e Antônio Corró o carroceiro que mais tarde ensinaria Mestre Paraná. Juntos, eles formavam uma comunidade de praticantes da classe trabalhadora que mantiveram a capoeira viva durante os anos de proibição, reunindo-se no cais, no Mercado Santa Bárbara, e durante festivais como a celebração de Santa Luzia documentada por Mestre Noronha em 1922.

Como muitos de sua geração, o destino de Tico após o início do século XX é desconhecido. Ele permanece como um dos ancestrais anônimos—trabalhadores africanos e afro-brasileiros que preservaram e transmitiram a arte durante seus anos mais perigosos.

## Historical Context / Contexto Histórico

### Cais Dourado (Golden Wharf)

The Cais Dourado was a concentration point for capoeiristas in the late 19th century and first decades of the 20th century. Located in the Pilar district near the waterfront, it was a universe of workers—dock hands, porters, stevedores, saveiristas—who breathed the culture of sailors and practiced capoeira in their daily lives.

The area witnessed frequent clashes between capoeiristas and police under the persecution of authorities like José Alvaro Cova, while Major Cosme de Farias emerged as their prominent defender.

Notable capoeiristas associated with Cais Dourado:
- Besouro de Mangangá (arrived from Santo Amaro via saveiro)
- **Tico** (Julio dos Santos Menezes) - porter from Santo Amaro
- Pedro Porreta - fish seller
- Caboclinho (Alfredo Martins Teixeira) - saveirista
- Antônio Corró - cart driver
- Mata Escura, Manoel Thié, Marinheiro, Mangue Sêco
- Ignácio Loyola, Pedro Piroca, Pedro Mineiro
- Antonio Boca de Porco, Gabiano, Agripino
- Paulo Gomes, José Martins, Cypriano, Três Pedaços
