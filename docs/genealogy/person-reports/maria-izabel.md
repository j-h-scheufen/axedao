## Biography / Biografia

### English

Maria Izabel's name survives in a single newspaper record: on December 2, 1914, the Gazeta do Povo of Salvador reported her arrest for street disorder while carrying a knife.

She is one of hundreds of women whose stories were uncovered by historian Paula Juliana Foltran during her doctoral research at the Arquivo Público do Estado da Bahia. Foltran's thesis, "Mulheres incorrigíveis: capoeiragem, desordem e valentia nas ladeiras da Bahia (1900-1920)" (2019), identified countless women like Maria Izabel - arrested for fighting in the streets, carrying weapons, and refusing to submit to the social order that sought to control them.

These women navigated the same urban geography as male capoeiristas: the streets, the markets, the docks, the ladeiras (hillside streets) of Salvador. They carried the same weapons - navalha (razor), faca (knife), canivete (penknife) - and engaged in the same culture of valentia (bravery/toughness) that defined the capoeiragem of their era.

Maria Izabel's profile in this genealogy serves as a memorial to these hundreds of anonymous women. While figures like Angélica Endiabrada, Salomé, and Maria Doze Homens achieved legendary status through oral tradition and literatura de cordel, most women who fought in the streets of old Bahia left only traces in police records and newspaper crime reports.

Foltran's research demonstrates that the erasure of these women from capoeira history was not due to their absence, but to the systematic way their participation was dismissed. When men fought in the streets, it was valentia; when women did the same, it was merely desordem (disorder). The same acts that built the reputations of male valentões were used to criminalize and forget the women who stood beside them.

Maria Izabel's 1914 arrest record proves she was there. Her inclusion here honors not just her, but all the unnamed women of her generation who helped forge the culture of capoeiragem in the ladeiras da Bahia.

## The Archival Record / O Registro Arquivístico

### English
**Gazeta do Povo, December 2, 1914**

Maria Izabel was arrested for "desordem" (disorder) in the streets of Salvador while carrying a knife. This single newspaper record is all that remains of her story.

The Gazeta do Povo, along with the Jornal de Notícias and Diário de Notícias, were the main newspapers that recorded police activity in early 20th century Salvador. These archives, preserved at the Arquivo Público do Estado da Bahia and the hemeroteca of the Instituto Geográfico e Histórico da Bahia (IGHB), contain countless similar records of women arrested for street fighting.

## Why This Profile Matters / Por Que Este Perfil Importa

### English
Maria Izabel represents the "dark matter" of capoeira history - the hundreds of practitioners whose names appear only in arrest records, never achieving the legendary status of an Angélica Endiabrada or a Salomé.

Paula Juliana Foltran's thesis definitively dismantled the myth that capoeira was exclusively a male domain. Her research proved that women were always there - fighting, carrying weapons, refusing to submit. But without oral traditions to preserve their memory, without songs to immortalize their names, most were forgotten.

This genealogy includes Maria Izabel not because we know her story, but precisely because we don't. She stands for all the "mulheres incorrigíveis" - the incorrigible women - whose presence in the streets of old Bahia was documented only to be dismissed as disorder.

### Português
Maria Izabel representa a "matéria escura" da história da capoeira - as centenas de praticantes cujos nomes aparecem apenas em registros de prisão, nunca alcançando o status lendário de uma Angélica Endiabrada ou uma Salomé.

A tese de Paula Juliana Foltran desmontou definitivamente o mito de que a capoeira era um domínio exclusivamente masculino. Sua pesquisa provou que as mulheres sempre estiveram lá - lutando, portando armas, recusando-se a se submeter. Mas sem tradições orais para preservar sua memória, sem canções para imortalizar seus nomes, a maioria foi esquecida.

Esta genealogia inclui Maria Izabel não porque conhecemos sua história, mas precisamente porque não conhecemos. Ela representa todas as "mulheres incorrigíveis" - cuja presença nas ruas da velha Bahia foi documentada apenas para ser descartada como desordem.
