## Biography / Biografia

### English

Alberto was an informal street capoeira practitioner active in the Parque dos Ferreiras neighborhood of Duque de Caxias in the early 1970s. Along with Jaime and Rubens, he taught young Daniel Canedo the fundamentals of capoeira in the streets before Daniel would later join the formal academy of Mestre Josias da Silva.

This informal street training was a common entry point for young capoeiristas in urban Rio de Janeiro during this period. While the formal academies of Mestre Barbosa (Academia Líder, founded 1970) and Mestre Josias (Associação de Capoeira Josias da Silva) were establishing structured capoeira instruction in Duque de Caxias, practitioners like Alberto operated in parallel, teaching in neighborhoods and preparing young people for the street rodas held in central Caxias.

The early 1970s were a transformative period for capoeira in the Baixada Fluminense. Mestre Barbosa had founded his association in 1970, Mestre Josias was operating his academy, and in June 1973 the famous Roda Livre de Caxias would be established at Praça do Pacificador—a street roda that continues to this day as one of Brazil's most important capoeira traditions. Alberto and his informal teaching companions were part of the broader grassroots culture from which this tradition emerged.

No further biographical details about Alberto have been documented. His legacy survives through Daniel Canedo, who went on to become a mestre and pioneer of capoeira in Canavieiras, Bahia, training a generation of mestres including Lucas, Barriga, Naval, and Isaque, and co-founding the Associação de Capoeira Adeptos de Zumbi (ACAZ) in 1993.

**HISTORICAL CONTEXT:**
In early 1970s Duque de Caxias, capoeira existed in two parallel worlds:
1. **Formal academies:** Mestre Barbosa (Academia Líder, founded 1970) and Mestre Josias (Associação de Capoeira Josias da Silva) provided structured training
2. **Informal street practice:** Practitioners like Alberto, Jaime, and Rubens who taught in neighborhoods and prepared youth for street rodas

Daniel Canedo's path was typical for the era: informal street training prepared him for rodas in central Caxias, then he joined a formal academy (Josias) where he was baptized and trained to become a mestre himself.

**RODA LIVRE DE CAXIAS:**
The famous Roda Livre de Caxias was founded on June 13, 1973, by youth from the Barbosa and Josias academies (including Mestres Russo and Peixe). This street roda represented a break from the academy model and continues today as Intangible Cultural Heritage of Duque de Caxias (2016). Alberto's informal teaching was part of the broader street capoeira culture that fed into this tradition.
