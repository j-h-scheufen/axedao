# Case Study: Escola de Capoeira Angola Nzinga (Germany)

**Date**: 2025-11-20
**Founded**: 1985 (Hamburg, Germany)
**Founder**: Mestre Paulo Siqueira
**Location**: Hamburg, Hannover, Kiel, Germany (3+ confirmed locations)
**Style**: Capoeira Angola / Contemporânea (Filhos de Angola lineage)
**Scale**: Regional (Northern Germany)

---

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

**Limited Scope**: This case study examines ONE group out of hundreds (possibly thousands) of Capoeira organizations worldwide.

**Not Representative**: Observations here reflect a regional European school led by a single pioneer mestre. This is fundamentally different from the large international networks previously studied. Many other organizational models exist.

**Preliminary Research**: All insights are preliminary and based on publicly available information from web searches, social media, and public websites. Community verification is essential before drawing any general conclusions about Capoeira organizational patterns.

**Name Disambiguation**: There are **TWO different groups** using the name "Nzinga":
1. **Escola de Capoeira Angola Nzinga** (Germany) - Led by Mestre Paulo Siqueira - website: capoeuropa.org (focus of this case study)
2. **Grupo Nzinga de Capoeira Angola** (Brazil/International) - Led by Mestra Janja, Mestra Paulinha, Mestre Poloca - website: nzinga.org.br

This case study focuses **exclusively** on Mestre Paulo Siqueira's German organization.

## Founder: Mestre Paulo Siqueira

### Biography

**Full Name**: Paulo Siqueira

**Born**: May 6, 1955, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

**Childhood Neighborhoods**:
- North Zone: Bonsucesso (lived with mother), Ramos (paternal grandfather), Olaria, Penha
- South Zone: Flamengo, Botafogo (maternal grandfather)

**Current Base**: Hamburg, Germany (since 1985, 40+ years)

**Known As**: "Siqueira" (stage nickname adopted during theatrical career)

### Early Exposure to Capoeira

**First Encounter**: Witnessed capoeira practitioners at a Portela samba school gathering in Candelária before a Presidente Vargas parade

**Introduction to Training** (~1972): Friend Rubens, a fireman from Cantagalo shantytown, introduced him to Mestre Roque's academy on Morro do Pavão (Pavãozinho favela between Ipanema and Copacabana)

**Training Environment**: According to Paulo Siqueira, the capoeira school was a real "quilombo" (community of resistance) in the most expensive area of the city - emphasizing the spontaneous, community-based capoeira distinct from formal South Zone academies

### Training Lineage

**Primary Teachers** (Morro do Pavão, early 1970s):
1. **Mestre Roque** (Roque Mendes dos Santos) - Supervising master, founder of Filhos de Angola
2. **Adilson "Camisa Preta"** (Adilson, born 1952) - Senior student who became Paulo's direct teacher

**Training Progression**:
- Began around 1972 under both Mestre Roque and Adilson
- As he advanced, Adilson ceased charging fees and incorporated him into performances
- Worked alongside Mestre Adilson Camisa Preta in theatrical productions
- Performed at venues including "Bahia Saravá" show at Almir Sinclair's establishment
- Performed at Intercontinental Hotel in Vidigal

**Title Granting**: After years of training, **Mestre Adilson** awarded Paulo the title of **Mestre**

**Note**: Adilson was Paulo's only mestre until Adilson's death in the 1990s

### Theatrical & Performance Training

**Classical Dance Training**: Studied under **Mercedes Baptista**, the first Black dancer at Rio's Municipal Theatre

**Mercedes Baptista's Significance**:
- Pioneer of Afro-Brazilian ballet
- First Black woman in Theatro Municipal do Rio de Janeiro's corps de ballet (1948)
- Created Ballet Folclórico Mercedes Baptista (1953)
- Developed choreographic codification of Afro-Brazilian dances

**Purpose**: Learning classical ballet technique alongside capoeira to develop theatrical performance skills and versatility

**Impact**: Prepared Paulo for professional performance career integrating capoeira with dance and theater

## Move to Europe & Group Founding

### Journey to Germany

**1980** - Initial Arrival:
- Traveled to Germany under contract
- Hired by a martial arts academy to teach capoeira classes
- Producer "Herr Ulm" based in Hannover
- Teaching group of fifteen students

**Touring Period** (1980-1985):
- Nine months with traveling group **Festival do Brasil**
- Performed throughout Europe
- Gained exposure to European audiences

**1985** - Permanent Settlement:
- **Hired by**: German woman who owned a theater called **Zeppelin** in Hamburg
- **Offer**: Teaching position + place to live in the theater
- **Decision**: Settled permanently in Hamburg
- **Action**: Founded his own group, **Nzinga**
- **Expansion**: Also began teaching in Hannover and Kiel

### Name Choice: "Nzinga"

**Historical Reference**: Named after **Queen Nzinga Mbandi Ngola** (1581-1663)

**Queen Nzinga's Significance**:
- Queen of Matamba and Angola (modern-day Angola)
- Led four-decade resistance against Portuguese occupation and slave trade
- Symbol of African resistance and freedom
- "Ngola" was the Ndongo name for the ruler (etymological root of "Angola")

**Connection to Capoeira Angola**:
- Represents African roots of the art form
- Symbolizes resistance to oppression
- Common name in Angola-focused capoeira groups worldwide
- Emphasizes cultural heritage and anti-colonial struggle

**Note**: While Paulo Siqueira's specific reasoning for choosing this name in 1985 is not documented in available sources, the name clearly connects to Capoeira Angola's African heritage and resistance philosophy

## The Capoeira Sommer-Meeting: A European Landmark Event

### Founding and Vision

**1987** - Launch of Capoeira Sommer-Meeting:
- Initiated by Mestre Paulo Siqueira in Hamburg
- **Format**: Week-long intensive event (Monday through Sunday)
- **Components**: Workshops, lectures, and rodas
- **Description**: "A groundbreaking week-long capoeira event that combined workshops, lectures, and rodas"

### Scale and Significance

**Participation**:
- 300-600 capoeiristas annually from across Europe
- Participants from all European countries
- **Brazilian mestres** invited from Europe and Brazil

**Closing Event**:
- Held at Hamburg's "Fabrik" venue
- Drew up to 600 attendees
- Public performance showcasing event

**European Significance**:
- **"One of the longest Capoeira-Treffen Europas"** (one of the longest capoeira gatherings in Europe)
- **Largest capoeira event outside Brazil** at the time
- **One of the first major capoeira events in Europe**

### Duration and Impact

**Operational Period**: 1987-2004 (17 years)

**Annual Frequency**: Every year from 1987 to 2004

**Impact**:
- Substantially elevated European capoeira standards
- Created network of European capoeiristas
- Provided access to Brazilian mestres for European students
- Established Hamburg as a major capoeira center in Europe
- Set precedent for large-scale European capoeira events

**Legacy**:
- Recognized as "one of the most important capoeira events in Europe during the late 20th century"
- Contributed significantly to establishing organized, large-scale capoeira instruction in Northern Europe

**End**: Event concluded in 2004 after 17 successful years

## Cultural Practices & Philosophy

### Style Classification

**Primary Style**: Capoeira Angola / Contemporânea

**Lineage-Based Classification**:
- Mestre Roque represents "one of the founding lineages of **capoeira contemporânea** in Rio de Janeiro"
- Mestre Roque's style: "Line of São Bento" - faster than other Angola styles
- Roots in Bahia Angola tradition (Besouro → Cobrinha Verde)
- Evolved in Rio favela context (Filhos de Angola)

**What is Contemporânea?**:
- Not purely Angola (though rooted in Angola tradition)
- Not Regional (Bimba's systematized style)
- Blend that emerged in Rio de Janeiro
- Faster, more dynamic than traditional Angola
- Community-based, less formalized than Regional
- Represents Rio's unique capoeira culture

**Group's Self-Description**:
- Official name includes "Angola" (Escola de Capoeira **Angola** Nzinga)
- Website describes as "Capoeira Angola"
- Reflects Angola roots and philosophy
- Teaching likely includes Angola traditions while incorporating contemporânea elements

**Website Philosophy Statement**:
"A combination from multiple cultural expressions: dance, music, combat, theater, play and spiritual practice."

### Teaching Philosophy

**Holistic Approach**:
- Individual and collective growth through partner interaction
- Music as integral component
- The roda (circle) as central practice
- "Discussions and conversations" alongside physical training
- Multiple cultural expressions integrated

**Training Components** (from Hamburg schedule):
- **Music instruction**: Instruments, songs, rhythms
- **Foundational training**: Basic movements and techniques
- **Conditioning**: Physical fitness and preparation
- **Sequencias**: Fixed and free movement sequences
- **Maculele**: Traditional stick dance from Afro-Brazilian culture
- **Roda**: Practice circle with live music

**Cultural Integration**:
- Not just martial art or sport
- Theater and performance (Mestre Paulo's background)
- Spiritual practice
- Community gathering
- Cultural transmission

### Community-Based Capoeira

**Mestre Paulo's Description of Training Environment**:
- "The capoeira school was a real **quilombo** in the most expensive area of the city"
- **Quilombo**: Historic term for communities of escaped enslaved Africans; symbol of resistance and freedom
- Emphasizes community over commercialization
- Favela-based training (Morro do Pavão) in wealthy South Zone area
- Social resistance and cultural preservation

**European Context**:
- Bringing this community-based approach to Germany
- Maintaining Brazilian cultural traditions abroad
- Creating spaces for cultural exchange
- European students learning Afro-Brazilian heritage

### Accessibility

**Trial Classes**: Free trial sessions at all locations

**Venue Partnerships**:
- Community centers
- School sports halls
- University programs (Kiel)
- Cultural theaters (original Zeppelin location)

**Approach**: Welcoming newcomers, encouraging observation and questions

## Key Takeaway

**Escola de Capoeira Angola Nzinga represents "first-generation diaspora transmission" model**:

### What Makes This Group Unique

1. **Pioneer Status**: Among the very first to bring capoeira to Europe (1980s)
2. **Diaspora Founder**: Mestre himself emigrated and established school abroad (not student continuation)
3. **Major Event Creation**: Organized Europe's largest capoeira event for 17 years (1987-2004)
4. **Regional Concentration**: Limited geographic expansion by choice, maintaining quality over scale
5. **Single-Leader Multi-City**: Founder personally teaches in all locations, travels between cities
6. **Cultural Bridge**: Direct transmission of Rio favela capoeira tradition to Northern Europe
7. **Theatrical Background**: Only mestre studied with significant classical dance/performance training
8. **Contemporânea in Europe**: Rare example of Rio contemporânea style (not Regional or pure Angola) established abroad
9. **Historical Documentation**: Writing book on European capoeira history from primary source perspective
10. **40-Year Consistency**: Four decades in Hamburg, continuous commitment to cultural transmission

### This Model Works Because

1. **Pioneer Advantage**: Established when competition was minimal
2. **Major Event Legitimacy**: Sommer-Meeting built reputation and network
3. **Personal Teaching**: Direct transmission from mestre maintains quality
4. **Regional Focus**: Manageable scale for single teacher
5. **Cultural Mission**: Clear purpose beyond commercial success
6. **Theatrical Skills**: Performance background aids in cultural transmission and events
7. **Community Partnerships**: Venue relationships reduce overhead
8. **European Context**: Meeting demand for authentic Brazilian cultural education

### Data Model Must Capture

1. **Diaspora founder** (different from student establishing autonomous group)
2. **Major event as core strategy** (not just supplement)
3. **Regional multi-city model** with traveling single leader
4. **First-generation European capoeira** (pioneer status)
5. **Theatrical/performance credentials** of founder
6. **Style identification vs. lineage reality** (contemporânea identified as angola)
7. **Event lifecycle** (17-year major event, then concluded)
8. **Cultural documentation projects** (book in progress)
9. **Free trial accessibility model**
10. **Venue partnership approach** (schools, youth centers, sports centers)

### Historical Significance

Mestre Paulo Siqueira and Escola de Capoeira Angola Nzinga represent the **first wave of European capoeira establishment** - the pioneers who brought the art to a continent where it was unknown. Without this first generation, the second generation (students training in Brazil and returning to Europe to teach) would not have had the foundation to build upon. The Capoeira Sommer-Meeting created networks and standards that shaped European capoeira for decades.

This is a **foundation story** for European capoeira history.
