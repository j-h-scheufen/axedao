# Case Study: Grupo Capoeira Filhos de Angola (GCFA)

**Date**: 2025-11-19
**Founded**: March 20, 1984
**Location**: Salvador, Bahia, Brazil (international presence)
**Style**: Capoeira Angola (traditional)
**Scale**: International (Brazil, Sweden, England, France, Spain, Switzerland, Germany, Greece - 25+ núcleos in Europe and Asia)

---

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

**Limited Scope**: This case study examines ONE group out of hundreds (possibly thousands) of Capoeira organizations worldwide.

**Not Representative**: Observations here reflect only GCFA's specific structure and history. Other groups may have completely different organizational models.

**Preliminary Research**: All insights are preliminary and based on publicly available information. Community verification is essential before drawing any general conclusions about Capoeira organizational patterns.

**Name Disambiguation**: There are at least TWO groups named "Filhos de Angola":
1. **Grupo Capoeira Filhos de Angola (GCFA)** - Founded 1984 in Salvador by Roberval, Laércio, Cabore, Rosalvo (THIS case study)
2. **Grupo Filhos de Angola** - Founded 1960 in Rio de Janeiro by Mestre Roque (different group, not covered here)

## Co-Founder Separation & Current Structure

### The Split

**Timeline**:
- **1984**: Four co-founders establish GCFA in Salvador
- **1990s**: Founders began operating in different countries/directions
- **1997**: Both Mestre Roberval (Stockholm) and Mestre Rosalvo (Berlin) established European operations
- **Present**: Mestre Laércio coordinates GCFA international network

**Documentation**: "Over time, the founding masters separated creating other groups, and Mestre Laercio has continued with Filhos de Angola which he directs today"

### Current Organizational Divisions

**Three apparent branches/continuations**:

1. **GCFA International Network** (Mestre Laércio)
   - Base: Berlin, Germany
   - Structure: 25+ núcleos in Europe and Asia
   - Model: Nucleus network (coordinated by Laércio)
   - Active locations confirmed

2. **GCFA Sverige** (Mestre Roberval)
   - Base: Stockholm, Sweden
   - Founded: 1997 as non-profit organization
   - Members: ~60 in Sweden, part of ~400 total GCFA members worldwide
   - Appears to remain under GCFA umbrella
   - Website: mestreroberval.com
   - Also active in: England, France, Spain, Switzerland, Germany

3. **Academia Jangada** (Mestre Rosalvo)
   - Base: Berlin, Germany (Kulturbrauerei, Prenzlauer Berg)
   - Founded: 1997 (first European Capoeira Angola academy)
   - Students: ~100-150 regularly training
   - Website: jangada.com
   - **Appears independent** from GCFA network (no GCFA affiliation mentioned on website)
   - Former students now teaching throughout Europe

4. **Mestre Cabore**: Unknown current activity/location

### Organizational Model

**Nucleus Network** (similar to Angoleiros do Sertão, Ngoma):
- Central coordinator (Mestre Laércio)
- Regional núcleos led by local instructors
- Cultural association structure (non-profit)
- No centralized belt system (traditional Angola approach)
- Local autonomy with shared identity

## Style: Capoeira Angola (Traditional)

### Lineage

**Multiple Angola lineages** converging in GCFA founders:

```
Mestre Pastinha
├─ Mestre Caiçara
│   └─ Mestre Laércio (primary teacher from 1978)
├─ Mestre João Pequeno
│   └─ Influenced Laércio, named Rosalvo as Mestre (2002)
├─ Mestre João Grande
│   └─ Influenced Laércio, named Rosalvo as Mestre (2002)
├─ Mestre Canjiquinha
│   └─ Trained Laércio
├─ Mestre Valdemar
│   └─ Trained Laércio, ABCA co-founder
├─ Mestre Boca Rica
│   └─ Trained Laércio, ABCA co-founder
├─ (Many other Angola mestres)

Mestre Cobra Mansa (Cobrinha)
└─ Mestre Rosalvo (from age 15)
    └─ Named Rosalvo as Contramestre (1991)
```

**Not a single-lineage tree**: GCFA founders trained under MULTIPLE Angola mestres, representing a convergence of traditional Angola knowledge rather than single-line transmission.

### Philosophy & Approach

**Traditional Preservation**:
- Founded ABCA (1991) specifically to preserve traditional Capoeira Angola roots
- "Art, knowledge, philosophy and awareness of Capoeira Angola"
- No belt/cord system (traditional Angola approach, not graded hierarchy)
- Emphasis on oral traditions and cultural transmission

**Cultural Values**:
- Interculturality
- Collective work
- Citizenship
- Creativity
- Connection to African roots and Afro-Brazilian culture

**Teaching Approach**:
- Comprehensive: not just physical movements but cultural knowledge and philosophy
- "Dynamic manner" of teaching
- Preservation AND development (not static tradition)

## Research Methodology Notes

### Guide Improvements Identified During This Study

**Guide Improvement #1: Name Disambiguation**
- **Issue**: Found two groups named "Filhos de Angola" (Mestre Roque 1960 Rio vs GCFA 1984 Salvador)
- **Fix**: Add disambiguation step in Phase 1 when multiple groups share the same name
- **Recommendation**: Include founding year, location, and primary founder in initial searches

**Guide Improvement #2: Conflicting Dates**
- **Issue**: ABCA founding cited as both 1987 and 1991 in different sources
- **Fix**: Document ALL conflicting dates with sources rather than choosing one arbitrarily
- **Recommendation**: Flag for community verification

**Guide Improvement #3: JavaScript-Heavy Websites**
- **Issue**: Berlin website (filhosdeangola.eu) was JavaScript-rendered, WebFetch extracted only framework code
- **Fix**: Note this limitation in guide, recommend alternative sources
- **Recommendation**: Try social media or affiliated núcleos' websites as backup

**Guide Improvement #4: Co-Founder Separation**
- **Issue**: Multiple co-founders may separate over time, continuing under same name or independently
- **Fix**: Add specific research step to investigate whether co-founders stayed together or separated
- **Recommendation**: Search for "[Founder Name] + [Group Name]" individually to identify divergent paths
