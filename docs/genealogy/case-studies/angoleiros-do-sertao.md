# Case Study: Angoleiros do Sertão

**Date**: 2025-11-19
**Founded**: 1980s
**Location**: Feira de Santana, Bahia, Brazil (20+ nucleus worldwide)
**Style**: Capoeira Angola
**Scale**: International (Brazil, Germany, Finland, Croatia, Colombia, USA)

---

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

**Limited Scope**: This case study examines ONE group out of hundreds (possibly thousands) of Capoeira organizations worldwide.

**Not Representative**: Observations here reflect only Angoleiros do Sertão's specific structure and history. Other Angola groups may have completely different organizational models.

**Preliminary Research**: All insights are preliminary and based on publicly available information. Community verification is essential before drawing any general conclusions about Capoeira organizational patterns.

**No Universal Patterns**: Angoleiros do Sertão's "nucleus" model appears to differ from other Angola groups studied, but this does not mean it represents a broader pattern. Further research across diverse group types is required.

## Founding History

### Founder: Mestre Cláudio Costa (Cláudio da Feira)

**Born**: March 13, 1966, Feira de Santana, Bahia, Brazil

**Early influences**:
- **Uncle Domingos**: Capoeira player
- **Uncle Roque**: Berimbau craftsman
- Watched rodas at local fairs while his mother sold acarajé (traditional street food)

**Training**:
- Initially trained at the school of **Mestre Di Mola**
- Primarily learned through **street experience** in Feira de Santana
- Later connected with renowned Capoeira Angola mestres in Bahia

**Lineage**: Mestre Di Mola → Mestre Cláudio Costa (plus street/community learning)

**Founded**: **Escola de Capoeira Angoleiros do Sertão** in the 1980s

### Name Significance

**"Angoleiros do Sertão"** = "Capoeira Angola Players of the Backlands"

- **Angoleiros**: Practitioners of Capoeira Angola
- **Sertão**: Backlands/interior region of Brazil (specifically northeastern interior)

**Geographic identity**: References the rural, interior regions of Bahia rather than coastal cities like Salvador. This is significant because:
1. Most Angola groups are associated with Salvador (urban center)
2. "Sertão" emphasizes rural, traditional, interior identity
3. Reflects Mestre Cláudio's origins in Feira de Santana (inland city, not coastal)

**Similar to Ngoma's naming**: Political/cultural statement about identity and roots (Ngoma = African word, Sertão = rural Brazilian interior)

## Organizational Structure

### "Nucleus" Model (Núcleo)

**20+ núcleos (nuclei) worldwide** operating as semi-autonomous branches

**Key characteristic**: Each nucleus maintains connection to Mestre Cláudio and the original tradition while operating locally.

**Structure appears to be**:
- **Headquarters**: Feira de Santana, Bahia (Mestre Cláudio)
- **Núcleos**: Local branches led by advanced students (Treinels, Professores)
- Each nucleus maintains the "Angoleiros do Sertão" name and identity
- Regular events where núcleos gather together ("Encontros")

### Leadership & Hierarchy

**Mestre Cláudio Costa**: Founder and primary leader

**Nucleus leaders** (examples):
- **Treinel Tito Casal**: Berlin nucleus (organized 2015, described as "long time student of Mestre Cláudio")
- Other núcleos led by students who trained in Feira de Santana

**Rank system** (incomplete data):
- **Mestre**: Mestre Cláudio
- **Treinel**: Intermediate rank (Tito Casal in Berlin) - between Professor and Mestre
- **Professor**: Teaching rank (not documented in sources)
- **Students**: Various graduation levels (not documented)

**Note**: "Treinel" rank is specific to some Angola groups, not universal across all Capoeira.

## Training Philosophy & Cultural Practices

### "Compromisso" (Commitment/Responsibility)

**Core philosophy**: Mestre Cláudio emphasizes "compromisso" - commitments and responsibilities that extend beyond the roda.

**Academic research**: Subject of scholarly articles examining the ethical practices and cultural philosophy of the group.

**Implications**:
- Capoeira as life commitment, not just sport
- Responsibilities to the group and community
- Cultural preservation as ethical duty

### Strict Discipline & Traditional Standards

**Mestre Cláudio's requirements**:

**Cleanliness and self-respect**:
- "You don't need to be rich to be clean; you just need self-respect"
- Students must maintain proper appearance and hygiene

**Musical excellence**:
- Must sing accurately (lyrics correct)
- Must clap rhythmically (timing precise)
- Must master all traditional instruments
- High standards for musical performance

**Traditional instruments**:
- Handcrafted from natural materials
- Emphasis on authentic construction methods

### Samba Rural Integration

**Unique characteristic**: Angoleiros do Sertão integrates **Samba Rural** (rural Afro-Brazilian samba) into regular practice.

**Saturday morning practice** (Feira de Santana):
- Play capoeira on wide median strip of main avenue
- Follow with rural style samba
- Public, open to community
- Weekly tradition

**Cultural preservation**: Emphasizes Afro-Brazilian culture beyond capoeira itself:
- Physical expression
- Traditional rhythms
- Music and dance
- Philosophy and heritage

### Collaboration: Music Album (2004)

**"Angoleiros do Sertão e do Recôncavo"**:
- Collaboration between Mestre Cláudio and **Mestre Felipe de Santo Amaro**
- Album of traditional capoeira songs
- Includes: ladainhas, corridos, quadras
- Available on Spotify and Apple Music

**"Recôncavo"**: Different region of Bahia (coastal area around Salvador)

**Significance**: Collaboration between different regional Angola traditions (Sertão interior + Recôncavo coastal)

## Expansion Model & Núcleo Organization

### How Núcleos Are Established

**Berlin example** (best documented):
- Organized 2015 by **Treinel Tito Casal**
- Tito described as "long time student of Mestre Cláudio in Brazil"
- Trained in Feira de Santana, then established Berlin nucleus
- Maintains connection through Mestre Cláudio's visits (workshops)

**Pattern suggests**:
1. Student trains with Mestre Cláudio in Feira de Santana
2. Student reaches advanced rank (Treinel, Professor, etc.)
3. Student establishes nucleus in new location (with approval?)
4. Nucleus operates semi-autonomously but maintains identity
5. Mestre Cláudio visits for workshops/events
6. Núcleos participate in annual "Encontros" (gatherings)

### "Encontros" (Gatherings)

**Annual or regular events** where núcleos come together:
- "Encontro dos Angoleiros do Sertão" in various cities
- Example: Berlin 10 years anniversary (2025) - suggests Berlin nucleus founded 2015
- São Sebastião hosts annual gathering
- Brasília hosts gatherings (January 2025 event documented)

**Function**:
- Maintain connections between dispersed núcleos
- Train together across locations
- Reinforce shared identity and tradition
- Opportunity for Mestre Cláudio to teach all students

### Social Media Strategy

**Decentralized social media presence**:
- **Official account**: @angoleirosdosertaoficial (Instagram)
- **Location-specific accounts**: Each nucleus maintains own social media
  - Brasília: 8,702 followers
  - São Paulo: 1,622 followers
  - Many locations have Facebook pages

**Contrast with other groups**:
- Not a single centralized account for all locations
- Each nucleus builds local community presence
- Maintains local identity while sharing overall Angoleiros do Sertão brand

## Comparison with Other Angola Groups

### Angoleiros do Sertão vs. Ngoma Capoeira Angola

| Aspect | Angoleiros do Sertão | Ngoma (Mestre Marrom) |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **Founded** | 1980s | 1990s (as Angola focus) |
| **Headquarters** | Feira de Santana (interior Bahia) | Morro da Babilônia, Rio (favela) |
| **Geographic identity** | "Sertão" (rural backlands) | Rio + African (Ngoma = African word) |
| **Scale** | 20+ núcleos, 6+ countries | Small, Berlin + Rio (+ Serbia via CapoeiraHub) |
| **Organizational model** | Nucleus network (semi-autonomous) | Personal transmission (autonomous continuation) |
| **Leadership** | Single mestre (Cláudio) with student-led núcleos | Single mestre (Marrom) |
| **Training philosophy** | Strict discipline, musical excellence, compromisso | Community-focused, donation-based, social justice |
| **Social media** | Decentralized (many location accounts) | Minimal (Facebook only) |
| **Public presence** | Weekly public rodas on main avenue | Favela-based, community-focused |
| **Expansion** | Structured núcleos with advanced students as leaders | Organic, students carry on tradition independently |
| **Cultural practices** | Samba Rural integration | Traditional Angola only |

**Key difference**: Angoleiros do Sertão has **intentional organizational structure** for expansion (nucleus model) while Ngoma has **organic personal transmission**.
