## Key Takeaway

**Capoeira Zoador represents a unique case study in the capoeira diaspora**:

1. **Academic Legitimacy**: Mestre Sucuri's M.A. in International Relations focusing on capoeira globalization provides scholarly grounding for his teaching, rare among capoeira mestres.

2. **Cultural Production**: First non-Brazilian to produce original capoeira music breaks traditional barriers and demonstrates diaspora innovation.

3. **Professional Model**: Operating as full-time professional school in Tokyo since 2003 demonstrates sustainability of capoeira outside Brazil in commercial context.

4. **Regional Focus**: Unlike many groups that expand internationally, Capoeira Zoador remains concentrated in Tokyo metropolitan area, representing a deliberately local approach.

5. **Critical Data Gap**: The inability to trace Mestre Sucuri's lineage to specific mestres or organizations highlights a challenge for genealogy databases: **not all practitioners have publicly documented training lineage**, especially in diaspora contexts where training may have occurred informally or across multiple groups.

**Data Model Implication**: The database must gracefully handle incomplete lineage information while still capturing valuable data about:
- Organizational models
- Cultural contributions
- Academic legitimacy
- Geographic presence
- Students and next generation
- Flagging nodes that require community verification

**For the Genealogy Tree Project**: Capoeira Zoador can be included as a "verified group" with "unverified lineage pending community input", demonstrating transparency about data limitations while preserving known information.

**Comparison Insight**: When compared to other non-Brazilian founders (Mestre Brabo in NZ, Mestre Cigano in Mexico), Mestre Sucuri's unclear lineage stands out. This may reflect:
- Different levels of public documentation
- Different cultural contexts (Japan vs. NZ vs. Mexico)
- Different emphases (academic/professional vs. community-building)
- Varying degrees of connection to capoeira establishment traditions
