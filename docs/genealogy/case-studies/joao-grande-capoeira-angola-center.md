# Case Study: Capoeira Angola Center of Mestre João Grande

**Date**: 2025-11-19
**Founded**: 1990 (New York City)
**Founder**: Mestre João Grande (João Oliveira dos Santos)
**Location**: Harlem, New York City, USA (international affiliates)
**Style**: Capoeira Angola (Traditional)
**Scale**: International (12+ locations across 9 countries)

---

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

**Limited Scope**: This case study examines ONE group out of hundreds (possibly thousands) of Capoeira organizations worldwide.

**Not Representative**: Observations here reflect only the Capoeira Angola Center of Mestre João Grande's specific structure and history. Other groups may have completely different organizational models.

**Preliminary Research**: All insights are preliminary and based on publicly available information. Community verification is essential before drawing any general conclusions about Capoeira organizational patterns.

**Historical Significance**: This case study is critically important because João Grande represents 1st generation lineage from Mestre Pastinha - the foundational figure of modern Capoeira Angola.

## Founding History

### The Founder - Mestre João Grande

**Birth**: January 15, 1933, Itagi, Bahia, Brazil

**Original Name**: João Oliveira dos Santos

**Nickname Origin**: Given by Mestre Pastinha to distinguish him from João Pequeno (João "Grande" = Big João, João "Pequeno" = Small João)

### Training with Mestre Pastinha (1953-1968)

**1953** (age 20): Joined Mestre Pastinha's academy
- Introduced to Pastinha by João Pequeno (who became his closest capoeira associate)
- João Pequeno had received João Grande from Mestre Barbosa

**1966**: Traveled to Senegal with Mestre Pastinha
- First World Festival of Black Arts in Dakar, Senegal
- International recognition of Capoeira Angola

**1968**: Received Diploma from Mestre Pastinha
- Officially became Mestre
- Pastinha's prophetic words (1970): "They [João Grande and João Pequeno] will be the great capoeiras of the future and for this I worked and fought with them and for them. They will be true masters, not improvised teachers"

### The Two Pastinha Heirs

**João Grande and João Pequeno** were Mestre Pastinha's "two most learned students" - the principal heirs to his legacy.

**Relationship**: Not biological brothers, but "capoeira brothers" (fellow students under the same master)

**Pastinha's Regard**: Both were students and afterwards contra-mestres of Mestre Pastinha, who said they would be the great Capoeiristas of the future

### Journey to the United States (1989-1990)

**1989**: Invited by Jelon Vieira to tour the United States
- Tour was tremendously successful
- Jelon Vieira had moved to NYC in 1975 and was one of the first to bring Capoeira to the US

**1990**: Returned for major performances
- National Black Arts Festival (Atlanta, Georgia)
- Schomburg Center for Research for Black Culture (New York City)
- Following these performances, decided to settle in NYC permanently

**1990**: Founded Capoeira Angola Center
- First location: 14th Street, downtown Manhattan
- Devoted to carrying forth the teachings of Mestre Pastinha
- Encouraged and supported by Mestre Jelon

**~2011**: Moved to current location
- 2916 Frederick Douglass Blvd (corner of 154th Street & 8th Avenue)
- 3rd floor, Harlem, New York, NY 10039
- Heart of Harlem neighborhood

## Lineage Chain - Foundational for Angola Tradition

### Generational Structure

**Generation 0**: **Mestre Pastinha** (1889-1981)
- "Father of Capoeira Angola"
- Legendary founder who systematized and preserved Angola tradition
- Founded Centro Esportivo de Capoeira Angola (1941)

**Generation 1**: **João Grande + João Pequeno**
- Direct students of Pastinha
- The "two most learned students" according to Pastinha
- Received formal diplomas from Pastinha (1968)
- **THIS IS THE FOUNDATIONAL GENERATION FOR MODERN ANGOLA LINEAGE**

**Generation 2**: Students of João Grande worldwide
- Mestres, Contra-mestres, Professors trained by João Grande
- International network spanning 9+ countries

### Why This Lineage Is Critical

**Shortest path to Pastinha**: All other groups studied so far are 2nd-5th generation from legendary mestres. João Grande is **1st generation from Pastinha himself**.

**Living Legend**: João Grande (92 years old as of 2025) represents a direct living link to the origins of modern Capoeira Angola.

**Pastinha's Words**: "Pastinha was my father, my grandfather, my everything in capoeira." - Mestre João Grande

## Teaching Philosophy and Cultural Preservation

### Core Philosophy

**"Capoeira is a game not about fighting, but about philosophy, culture, community, and playing together."** - Mestre João Grande

**Comprehensive Art**: "An art, a dance, a profession, and a culture"
- "A capoeirista is a dancer, a poet, a singer, and a philosopher"
- "It brings nothing but good for people's body and spirit"

**Way of Life**: Teaches "in the African style, as a way of life, as his teacher Mestre Pastinha did before him"

**Path of Knowledge**: Following Pastinha's teaching of Capoeira Angola as "a path of self knowledge and mastery"

### Teaching Methodology

**1. Nature-Based Learning**
- Constant reference to waves of the ocean
- Movement of different animals
- Shows students films of animals (especially monkeys doing cartwheels)

**2. Metaphorical Teaching**
- Uses poetic imagery to convey capoeira principles
- Nature metaphors throughout instruction

**3. Core Requirements**
- Must have a good teacher to guide you
- Must be able to play all instruments
- Must develop "jogo bonita" (beautiful game)

**4. Cultural Resistance**
- **Anti-Anglicization**: Resists anglicization because it weakens capoeira Angola's identity
- Maintains Portuguese language, Brazilian music, African cultural elements
- Preservation over adaptation

### Preservation Mission

**60+ Years of Dedication**: For over 60 years, João Grande has dedicated his life to preserving and sharing the rich tradition of capoeira angola

**Purpose of Academy** (official statement):
- Preserve and promote the traditions of Capoeira Angola
- Uphold the fundamental tenets of the Angoleiro
- Provide community-based support for Capoeiristas

**Cultural Roots**: Trains students in the tradition of Capoeira Angola which originated in central Africa and was brought to Brazil by enslaved people

**Trained Thousands**: Has trained thousands of students worldwide in authentic Pastinha tradition
