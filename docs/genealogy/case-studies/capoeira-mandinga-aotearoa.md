# Case Study: Capoeira Mandinga Aotearoa (New Zealand)

**Date**: 2025-11-19
**Founded**: 1992 (Auckland, New Zealand)
**Founder**: Mestre Brabo (Grant Cole)
**Parent Organization**: Capoeira Mandinga (Mestre Marcelo Caveirinha)
**Location**: New Zealand - Auckland, Taranaki, Wellington, Christchurch
**Style**: Capoeira Angola (Traditional) - evolved from Regional/Contemporânea roots
**Scale**: National (4 cities across New Zealand)

---

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

**Limited Scope**: This case study examines ONE regional chapter of the international Capoeira Mandinga organization, specifically the New Zealand (Aotearoa) branch.

**Not Representative**: Observations here reflect only Capoeira Mandinga Aotearoa's specific structure and history. Other Capoeira Mandinga chapters and other groups may have completely different organizational models.

**Preliminary Research**: All insights are preliminary and based on publicly available information. Community verification is essential before drawing any general conclusions about Capoeira organizational patterns.

**Historical Significance**: This case study is critically important because:
1. **First capoeira in New Zealand** - Pioneered capoeira in an entirely new country (1992)
2. **Diaspora founding by immigrant** - Founded by New Zealander who brought capoeira home from USA
3. **Style evolution documented** - Clear transition from Regional/Contemporânea to Angola (2004)
4. **First NZ mestre** - Mestre Brabo is New Zealand's first capoeira master (2007)
5. **Island nation expansion** - Shows how capoeira reaches remote Pacific locations

## Founding History - Bringing Capoeira to New Zealand

### The Founder - Mestre Brabo (Grant Cole)

**Birth Name**: Grant Cole
**Capoeira Name**: Brabo (means "tough" or "fierce")
**Current Title**: Mestre (achieved 2007)
**Nationality**: New Zealander (Aotearoa)

**Timeline**:
- **1983**: Trip to United States, met Marcelo Caveirinha
- **1984**: Began training with Mestre Marcelo
- **1991 (November)**: Returned to New Zealand
- **1992**: Founded Capoeira Mandinga Aotearoa (first capoeira in NZ)
- **1999**: Received title of Professor
- **2007**: Elevated to Mestre (New Zealand's first capoeira master)
- **2025**: 33+ years training relationship with Mestre Marcelo

### Discovery of Capoeira (1983)

**Context**: Grant Cole, a New Zealander, traveled to the United States in 1983.

**Meeting Mestre Marcelo**: During this trip, Cole met Marcelo Caveirinha (who later became his mestre). This chance encounter would change the trajectory of his life and introduce an entirely new art form to New Zealand.

**Training Begins (1984)**: Cole began formal training with Mestre Marcelo Pereira in 1984, becoming one of Mestre Marcelo's early international students.

### Return to Aotearoa (1991-1992)

**November 1991**: Cole returned to New Zealand after years of training in the United States.

**The Situation**: "There was no capoeira in Auckland. There was in fact, no capoeira in Aotearoa."
- New Zealand had zero capoeira presence
- No teachers, no schools, no community
- Cole was the only person in the country who knew the art

**Starting from Zero (1992)**:
- Cole started training "in the corner of a recreation centre in Auckland"
- Simple motivation: "having some people to play capoeira with"
- Founded Capoeira Mandinga Aotearoa
- Began teaching classes
- **This was the first capoeira ever taught in New Zealand**

### Growing Recognition

**Professor (1999)**: After 7 years leading Capoeira Mandinga Aotearoa, Cole received the title of Professor from Mestre Marcelo.

**Mestre (2007)**: On April 21, 2007, Cole was elevated to Mestre by Mestre Marcelo Caveirinha, becoming **New Zealand's first capoeira master**.

**Official Recognition**: The New Zealand news media (Scoop News) covered this achievement as a significant cultural milestone: "NZ's First Capoeira Master!"

## Lineage and Style Evolution

### Initial Lineage - Regional/Contemporânea (1984-2004)

**Generation Chain from Mestre Bimba**:

```
Mestre Bimba (1900-1974, "Father of Capoeira Regional")
    ↓
Mestre Suassuna (co-founded Cordão de Ouro, 1967)
    ↓
Mestre Marcelo Caveirinha (founded Capoeira Mandinga, 1995)
    ↓
Mestre Brabo (Grant Cole) - Generation 4 from Bimba
```

**Mestre Suassuna (Generation 2)**:
- Co-founded Associação de Capoeira Cordão de Ouro with Mestre Brasília
- September 1, 1967 founding date
- Trained many prominent mestres including Marcelo Caveirinha

**Mestre Marcelo Caveirinha (Generation 3)**:
- Born 1959 in São Paulo, Brazil
- Trained under Mestre Suassuna (CDO)
- One of the last generation to train directly with Mestre Suassuna
- **1984**: Left Brazil, moved to Oakland, California
- One of only three people teaching capoeira in USA at that time
- Founded Associação de Capoeira Senzala Grande (1984)
- **1995**: Changed name to Capoeira Mandinga
- International expansion: USA, New Zealand, Israel, China, Taiwan, Japan, Italy, Iceland, Mexico, Brazil

**Mestre Brabo (Generation 4)**:
- Training since 1984 with Mestre Marcelo
- Brought Capoeira Regional/Contemporânea tradition to New Zealand

### Style Transition to Angola (2004-Present)

**Around 2004**: Significant shift in organizational focus toward traditional Capoeira Angola.

**New Influences - Angola Masters**:

**1. Mestre João Pequeno (Generation 1 from Pastinha)**:
- Born 1917, died 2011
- Direct student of Mestre Pastinha
- One of two principal heirs to Pastinha's legacy (with João Grande)
- Re-founded CECA after Pastinha's death (1982)
- Mestre Brabo traveled to Brazil to train with João Pequeno

**2. Mestre Jogo de Dentro (Generation 2 from Pastinha)**:
```
Mestre Pastinha (1889-1981)
    ↓
Mestre João Pequeno (1917-2011)
    ↓
Mestre Jogo de Dentro (born 1965)
```

**About Mestre Jogo de Dentro**:
- Born April 6, 1965, Alagoinhas, Bahia
- Started capoeira 1982 at Forte Santo Antônio with João Pequeno
- Nicknamed by Mestre João Grande (1983): "Jogo de Dentro" = "Inside Game"
- Founded Semente do Jogo de Angola (September 9, 1990)
- Recognized as Mestre by João Pequeno (February 27, 1994)
- Author: "Capoeira Angola e Ancestralidade" (2020)
- "One of the great masters of capoeira angola today"

**Connection to Capoeira Mandinga Aotearoa**: Around 2004, the organization began cultivating ties with Mestre Jogo de Dentro, traveling to Bahia for training and bringing his Angola teachings back to New Zealand.

**3. Mestre Roxinho (ECAMAR Sydney)**:
```
Mestre Virgilio
    ↓
Mestre Roxinho (born 1969, Mestre 2002)
```

**About Mestre Roxinho**:
- Born 1969, Ilha de Veracruz, Bahia
- Edielson da Silva Miranda
- Founded ECAMAR (Escola de Capoeira Angola Mato Rasteiro) - 1998 Salvador
- Mestre title from Mestre Virgilio (2002)
- **2006**: Immigrated to Australia, established ECAMAR Sydney
- **First Bahia Angola master to establish school in Australia**
- Also founded Project Bantu (youth engagement through Angola)

**Connection to Capoeira Mandinga Aotearoa**: Mestre Roxinho's establishment of ECAMAR in Sydney (2006) proved highly influential. The website states he was "a key supporter and inspiration" for Capoeira Mandinga Aotearoa's continued evolution toward the capoeira angola tradition.

**Geographic Proximity**: Sydney to Auckland is only ~2,150 km, making it feasible for workshops and regular visits.

### Current Style (Post-2004)

**Official Statement**: "They train capoeira angola, a traditional form of the Brazilian artistic expression."

**Dual Lineage Recognition**:
- Organizational connection: Capoeira Mandinga (Mestre Marcelo)
- Stylistic practice: Capoeira Angola (João Pequeno, Jogo de Dentro, Roxinho lineages)
- This shows **style can evolve while maintaining organizational affiliation**

## Cultural Practices and Philosophy

### Teaching Approach

**Comprehensive Capoeira Angola**:
From website: "Classes on the movements, rhythms and traditions of Capoeira Angola"

**Components** (typical of Angola tradition):
1. **Physical Movement**: Traditional Angola groundwork, slow game, malícia
2. **Music**: All instruments (berimbau, pandeiro, atabaque, agogô, reco-reco)
3. **Song**: Portuguese lyrics, traditional corridos and ladainhas
4. **Cultural Education**: History, African roots, Brazilian culture
5. **Philosophy**: Community, respect, tradition preservation

### Influences on Philosophy

**From Mestre Marcelo** (Regional/Contemporânea background):
- Systematic teaching methodology
- International adaptability
- Organizational structure

**From Angola Masters** (João Pequeno, Jogo de Dentro, Roxinho):
- Traditional slow game
- Cultural preservation emphasis
- Spiritual/philosophical dimensions
- Community-centered practice
- Resistance to commercialization

### Community Building

**"Having some people to play capoeira with"**: Mestre Brabo's original motivation reflects community-first philosophy.

**National Organization**: Creating unified New Zealand capoeira community across four cities.

**All Levels Welcome**: "Classes of all levels for adults and children" - inclusive approach.

**Family Atmosphere**: Kids' classes in Auckland, Taranaki, Wellington suggest family-friendly environment.
